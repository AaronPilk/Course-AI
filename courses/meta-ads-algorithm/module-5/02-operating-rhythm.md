# Daily / weekly / monthly operating rhythm

## Why rhythm matters

Most account problems come from one of two failure modes: doing too much (over-tweaking and resetting learning) or doing too little (not refreshing creative, not auditing, letting hygiene rot). A defined rhythm prevents both.

The right rhythm separates work that should happen daily, weekly, and monthly. Each cadence has different goals and different metrics.

## Daily — monitor, don't tweak

What to do:

- Open Ads Manager once. Glance at: spend, CPA, ROAS, hook rate, frequency. 5 minutes.
- Look at 3+ day-old data; today's is incomplete.
- Note anything anomalous (CPA up 30%+ vs last week, frequency >7, ROAS broken).
- Document the anomaly with date and metric.

What NOT to do:

- Raise/lower budgets daily.
- Pause "underperforming" ads on day 1 — too early.
- Edit ad sets (any change resets learning).
- React to intraday spikes.

The daily check is for awareness, not action. Action lives at weekly cadence.

## Weekly — iterate creative, audit hygiene

A single 60-90 minute weekly session covers most operational needs:

**1. Creative refresh (30 minutes).**
- Launch 3-5 new ads (batched at the ad level inside existing ad sets).
- Pause 2-3 bottom-performing ads.
- Note which dimensions you varied (angle, format, creator, hook).
- Update test log.

**2. Performance review (15 minutes).**
- Read weekly CPA, ROAS, CTR, hook rate, frequency.
- Compare to prior week. What changed?
- Identify week's standout winner and standout loser.

**3. Audience hygiene (10 minutes).**
- Confirm exclusions still wired.
- Check retargeting frequency; expand window if >10.
- Refresh CRM upload if 30+ days stale.

**4. Budget decisions (10 minutes).**
- If CPA holding within target: consider 20-30% bump on best campaign.
- If CPA breaking: hold or pull back; not the time to scale.
- Decision per campaign, document the reasoning.

**5. Planning (10 minutes).**
- Next week's creative production needs.
- Anything to test in the upcoming week.
- Any structural change being considered (rarely).

This is the load-bearing cadence. Most of the value-add per dollar spent comes from doing this competently every week.

## Monthly — audit, plan, report

A longer 2-3 hour session once a month:

**1. Account audit (45 minutes).**
- Full hygiene pass (covered Module 2 Lesson 4).
- Audience overlap audit.
- Lookalike seed freshness.
- Domain verification still valid.
- AEM event priority still correct.
- Active campaigns: should anything be retired?

**2. Performance trend review (30 minutes).**
- 30-day vs prior 30-day metrics.
- Identify what's drifting (CPA up, hook rates down, frequency rising).
- Look at creative library age — anything past 90 days should be reviewed.

**3. Strategic decisions (30 minutes).**
- Are we hitting the goal for the month? If not, what's the gap?
- New angles or offers to test next month?
- Budget reallocation across products / channels?
- Anything ASC should take over from manual or vice versa?

**4. Stakeholder reporting (30 minutes).**
- Top 5 metrics with prior-month deltas.
- Key creative wins and losses.
- Spend efficiency vs target.
- Next month's priorities.

## Quarterly — incrementality, structural

Every 3 months, harder questions:

- Run a lift test on a suspected over-attributed channel (retargeting, branded search).
- Review attribution-vs-actual revenue mismatch.
- Audit account structure: campaigns to consolidate, kill, or split?
- Review creative pipeline: are we producing enough? What's the cost per ad?
- Review tool stack: CAPI implementation health, server-side tagging, MMM if applicable.

Most accounts skip quarterly review and only do daily/weekly. Performance erodes silently.

## Half-yearly — strategy

Every 6 months:

- Annual goal-to-date check.
- Budget reallocation across major channels (Meta vs Google vs TikTok vs other).
- New market expansion decisions.
- Big creative concept refresh (new angles, new creator partnerships).
- Team / agency / freelancer review.

## What changes by account size

The rhythm scales with spend:

- **Under $20k/mo:** weekly is enough; daily can be a quick glance every other day; monthly can be combined with quarterly.
- **$20k-$100k/mo:** daily glance, weekly session, monthly audit, quarterly lift test. This article's default cadence.
- **$100k-$500k/mo:** twice-weekly creative iteration, daily monitoring, monthly audit, monthly stakeholder report, quarterly lift.
- **$500k+/mo:** daily ops by team, weekly forecast/budget meeting, monthly leadership review, ongoing lift tests rolling.

Match the rhythm to spend and team size.

## Common rhythm mistakes

- **Daily tweaks** — most accounts' biggest leak. Hold the ad set steady.
- **Skipping weekly review** — performance decays without weekly intervention.
- **Monthly with no action** — auditing without changing anything is bookkeeping, not management.
- **Hero metrics instead of operating metrics** — reporting on "engagement rate" without checking CPA is theater.
- **No documentation of decisions** — re-litigating the same questions month over month.

## A simple operating doc

Maintain one shared doc (Notion, Google Doc, whatever) with:

1. **Targets**: monthly spend, CPA, ROAS by product/campaign.
2. **Test log**: every test, dates, dimensions varied, outcome, decision.
3. **Open questions**: things to test next, decisions pending.
4. **Weekly notes**: what changed, what we did, what we're watching.
5. **Monthly summary**: numbers, wins, losses, next month priorities.

Future-you and any teammate can read this and know where the account is in 5 minutes. The compound effect of disciplined documentation over 12 months is enormous.

## Roles when running a team

If multiple people touch the account:

- **Owner / strategist**: weekly review, monthly audit, quarterly direction.
- **Creative producer**: new ads pipeline (3-5/week).
- **Media buyer**: daily monitoring, weekly batch launches, hygiene.
- **Analyst** (at scale): tracking, lift tests, reporting.

Even a 1-person operation should mentally separate these roles by time of day. The strategist-hat and the buyer-hat make different decisions.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **No defined cadence** — work happens when it happens, badly.
- **Daily action**, weekly thinking — should be reverse.
- **Reporting without managing** — generating reports nobody acts on.
- **All-hands-on-fire weeks** — symptom of skipped earlier rhythms.

## Summary

- Daily: monitor only, no edits.
- Weekly: creative refresh, hygiene, budget decisions, planning. 60-90 minutes.
- Monthly: audit, trend review, strategic decisions, reporting. 2-3 hours.
- Quarterly: lift testing, structural review.
- Half-yearly: strategy and big-picture decisions.
- Documentation compounds; without it, you re-decide the same things.
