# Music and sync — the rhythm question

## What music does

Music is the emotional engine of edits. It:
- **Establishes tone.** Tense / sad / triumphant / playful.
- **Sets pace.** Tempo drives cut rhythm.
- **Smooths transitions.** Music bridges scenes that otherwise feel disjointed.
- **Holds attention.** Silence is harder to watch than music.
- **Signals importance.** Swells call attention to moments.

A great cut without music = good. A great cut with the right music = unforgettable.

## Where to find music

**Royalty-free libraries (subscription):**
- **Artlist.** Curated; premium feel.
- **Musicbed.** Premium licensing.
- **Soundstripe.** Subscription.
- **Epidemic Sound.** Massive catalog.
- **Storyblocks.** Stock-style.

**Free libraries:**
- **YouTube Audio Library** (free but limited).
- **Pixabay Music.**
- **Free Music Archive (FMA).**

**Commissioned:**
- Custom score from composer. For brand work; pricey but unique.

**Licensed commercial:**
- Specific songs (Beatles, Taylor Swift). Expensive; complicated.

For YouTube / corporate / commercial: subscription libraries dominate in 2026.

## License attention

Every music has licensing terms:
- **Subscription library.** Use within license terms (typically: covered for your channel/projects while subscription active).
- **Public domain.** Free for any use (classical music; old folk).
- **Creative Commons.** Specific conditions (BY = credit; SA = share-alike; NC = non-commercial).
- **Commercial license.** Per-use; expensive.

Use unlicensed music = monetization claims, takedowns, lawsuits. Worth zero shortcut.

## Picking music for an edit

Questions:
- **What tone?** Energetic / contemplative / melancholy / hopeful.
- **What pace?** Fast cuts need fast tempo; slow cuts need slow.
- **Has lyrics?** If yes, attention competes with dialogue; usually skip.
- **Has identifiable instruments?** Acoustic guitar = "casual"; orchestral = "epic"; synth = "modern."

Listen to 10 candidates; pick top 3; place each in edit briefly; commit.

## Music and dialogue

Two patterns:
1. **Music bed.** Continuous music underneath dialogue; lower volume during voice.
2. **Music + dialogue cuts.** Music plays in non-dialogue sections; silent (or ambient) during dialogue.

For YouTube interview / podcast: pattern 1 with Auto-Duck. For documentary / cinematic: pattern 2 sometimes (silence carries weight).

## Setting music level

Without dialogue: peak around -6 to -12 dB.

With dialogue: peak around -20 to -25 dB (10-15 dB below dialogue). Auto-Duck handles automatically.

For broadcast: music + dialogue mixed within 4-6 dB of each other usually; not the YouTube standard but standard for broadcast.

## Tempo and cut rhythm

A song at 120 BPM has 2 beats per second. Cuts on beats feel intentional.

For high-energy edits:
- Cut on every beat (fast).
- Cut on every other beat.
- Cut on the kick drum.

For slow cuts:
- Cut on phrase changes (every 4 / 8 / 16 bars).
- Cut on the breakdown.
- Let the song breathe.

Audacity / Premiere markers help: tap M on the timeline at each beat → mark the song; align cuts to markers.

## Audition mood matching

In Premiere: the Search Music panel can suggest music based on tempo, mood, genre.

For workflows with Adobe Stock Audio subscription: built-in search; preview; license; drop into timeline.

Cuts research time significantly.

## Music cuts (editing music)

Songs are 3-4 minutes; videos might need 90 seconds. Edit the song:
- **Cut to fit.** Remove a verse / chorus / bridge.
- **Crossfade between sections.** Smooth join (1-3 second crossfade).
- **Extend a loop.** Repeat a section.
- **Lift a stinger.** Use just the ending beat as a sting.

For YouTube intros: 8-15 second stings. For longer content: longer music edits.

## Music transitions

Between two pieces of music in a sequence:
- **Hard cut.** Abrupt; usually wrong unless mood shift is intentional.
- **Crossfade.** 1-3 seconds; smooth transition.
- **Drop to silence then up.** Punctuates mood change.

Crossfade is the default; transitions are dramatic exceptions.

## Sync to picture

For specific moments — explosion synced to bass hit, cut synced to drum:
- Mark the picture moment.
- Mark the audio moment.
- Slide the audio clip until they align.

Audio markers (Window → Markers panel) help track multiple sync points.

For music-driven edits (montages): build to the music; cuts and dramatic moments align to beats.

## Stings and accents

Short musical hits — stings — punctuate moments:
- "Today on the show..." → sting.
- Bullet-point text appears → sting.
- Cut to new scene → small sting.

Use sparingly; over-stinging is amateur. One sting per major moment; not every cut.

## Music-free zones

Dialogue requires attention; music competes. Some moments need music silence:
- Emotional confession in interview.
- Critical product demo voice-over.
- Punchline that needs to land.

Strip music; let voice carry alone; music returns. The silence emphasizes; the return resumes flow.

## Loops and ambience

For minimal music feel:
- **Drones / pads.** Continuous low background; less melodic.
- **Atmospheric loops.** Ambient music that feels like "space" not "song."

Good for documentary, contemplative content. Music presence without distraction.

## Foley and SFX rhythm

Beyond music, sound effects shape feel:
- **Whooshes** for transitions.
- **Hits** for impacts.
- **Risers** for build-up.
- **Foley** (footsteps, doors, cloth movement) for realism.

For dramatic edits: SFX layer adds polish.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Music too loud.** Drowns dialogue; auto-duck saves you.
- **Wrong tone.** Upbeat music over somber content; jarring.
- **Same song everywhere.** Repetitive within a series.
- **Untrimmed song.** Plays 3 minutes when the edit needs 90 seconds.
- **No license.** YouTube claims; takedown; legal risk.
- **Music with lyrics over dialogue.** Two voices competing.

## Summary

- Music shapes emotion, pace, transitions, attention.
- Subscription libraries (Artlist, Musicbed, Epidemic, Soundstripe) dominate for licensed content.
- Music bed (continuous) with Auto-Duck OR music between dialogue sections.
- Level: 10-15 dB below dialogue when both playing.
- Cut on beats / phrase changes for rhythmic edits.
- Crossfade between music pieces (1-3s); hard cut for mood shifts.
- Strip music for emotional dialogue moments; silence emphasizes.
- License every piece; unlicensed = problems.
