# Change orders + cost control

## What change orders are

Modification to contract:
- Scope addition or deletion.
- Cost adjustment.
- Schedule impact.

Signed by owner, architect, contractor.

For: contract amendment.

## Causes

- Owner-driven (preference changes).
- Design-driven (drawings error).
- Field condition (differing site).
- Code/regulatory.
- Substitutions.
- Acceleration.

For: source categories.

## Process

1. Discovery of need.
2. PCO (Pending Change Order) submitted.
3. Owner reviews.
4. Pricing negotiated.
5. Schedule impact assessed.
6. Approved CO issued (signed).
7. Work performed + paid.

Can take days to weeks.

For: typical flow.

## CO pricing

Cost-plus method:
- Direct labor + materials + equipment.
- Overhead markup (5-15%).
- Profit markup (5-15%).
- Insurance + bond if material.

Or unit price if pre-agreed in contract.

For: pricing math.

## RFI vs. CO

- **RFI.** Question without cost (usually).
- **CO.** When work changes + cost.

RFI may lead to CO if change required.

For: relationship.

## CO impact on schedule

Each CO:
- Time to negotiate.
- Time to perform.
- Sometimes acceleration required.

Track explicitly.

For: not just cost.

## Cost reports

Monthly:
- Budget by category.
- Actual cost-to-date.
- Committed (not yet spent but contracted).
- Estimated at completion (forecast).
- Variance.
- % complete.

Earned Value Management common.

For: tracking.

## Earned Value (EVM)

```
Budget at Completion (BAC) = total budget
Planned Value (PV) = % planned completed × BAC
Earned Value (EV) = % actually completed × BAC
Actual Cost (AC) = actual money spent

Schedule Variance: SV = EV - PV
Cost Variance: CV = EV - AC
Cost Performance Index: CPI = EV / AC
Schedule Performance Index: SPI = EV / PV
```

CPI < 1: over budget. SPI < 1: behind schedule.

For: project health.

## Buy-out

After contract signed, contractor "buys out" subcontracts:
- Lock prices.
- May get below bid estimate = profit.
- Or above = loss.

Significant 1-3 month effort.

For: post-contract phase.

## Payment timing

Standard:
- Pay application submitted month-end.
- Reviewed within 7 days.
- Paid within 30-60 days.

Cash flow gap = financing cost.

For: business reality.

## Lien waivers

Each payment:
- Conditional lien waiver (with submittal).
- Unconditional after payment received.

Required from contractor + subs + suppliers.

For: legal protection.

## Disputes

Common areas:
- Change order pricing.
- Schedule extensions.
- Quality acceptance.

Resolution:
- Negotiation.
- Mediation.
- Arbitration.
- Litigation.

For: when things go bad.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Verbal change orders.** Hard to enforce.
- **No cost reports.** Surprises at the end.
- **Late payment.** Subs walk off job.

## Summary

- Change orders modify contract; signed by all parties.
- Process: discovery → pricing → schedule → approval → execute.
- EVM tracks cost + schedule performance.
- Buy-out locks subcontract prices.
- Lien waivers + payment timing managed.
