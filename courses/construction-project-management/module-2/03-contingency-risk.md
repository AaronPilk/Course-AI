# Contingency + risk management

## What contingency is

Money reserved for known unknowns:
- Things will go wrong.
- Don't know which.
- Reserve a budget.

Typical:
- 5-10% for well-defined projects.
- 10-20% for moderate uncertainty.
- 20-30%+ for renovation/heavy uncertainty.

For: realistic budgeting.

## Owner contingency vs. contractor contingency

- **Owner contingency.** Owner's hidden buffer; not in contract.
- **Contractor contingency.** Within GMP; spent at contractor discretion or with approval.

Two separate buffers usually.

For: who controls reserve.

## Risk register

Document risks:
- Description.
- Probability.
- Impact (cost + schedule).
- Mitigation.
- Owner.

Updated weekly.

For: structured risk tracking.

## Risk matrix

Probability × Impact:
```
          Low  Med  High
Low       L    L    M
Med       L    M    H
High      M    H    H
```

Action by severity:
- L: monitor.
- M: mitigate.
- H: actively manage.

For: prioritization.

## Common risks

- **Weather delays.** Rain days, snow.
- **Material price spikes.** Lumber, steel volatility.
- **Subcontractor default.** Going out of business.
- **Differing site conditions.** Soil, utilities.
- **Design errors.** Drawings missing/wrong.
- **Permits/inspections.** Bureaucratic delays.
- **Labor strikes.** Union disputes.

For: hazard checklist.

## Differing site conditions

If excavation reveals:
- Rock instead of soil.
- Underground utilities not shown.
- Hazmat.

Contract type matters:
- DBB: contractor entitled to change order.
- DB/IPD: shared risk.

For: subsurface unknown.

## Liquidated damages risk

Quantify:
- Days late × LD rate.
- Owner-disclosed amount.

Hedge with schedule buffer.

For: schedule risk.

## Material escalation

Volatile commodities:
- Steel: 30%+ swings.
- Lumber: 50%+ swings (2020-2022).
- Copper, asphalt: also volatile.

Mitigations:
- Lock prices early.
- Escalation clauses in contract.
- Pre-purchase materials.

For: commodity hedging.

## Force majeure

Contract clause for:
- Acts of God.
- War.
- Pandemics.
- Government action.

Excuses delay; sometimes cost relief.

For: extreme events.

## Risk transfer

Methods:
- Insurance.
- Bonds.
- Subcontracts (push risk down).
- Owner-acceptance.
- Allowances.

For: getting risk off your books.

## Mitigations

Common:
- Buy out (lock subcontract prices).
- Pre-order long-lead items.
- Multiple supplier sources.
- Buffer schedule.
- Pre-construction surveys.
- Phased construction (early funding less risk).

For: practical reduction.

## Quantitative risk analysis

Software (Acumen, Primavera Risk):
- Monte Carlo simulation.
- 1000s of project iterations.
- P50/P80 estimate confidence levels.

For: data-driven contingency.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **No contingency.** Overrun guaranteed.
- **Wrong contingency size.** Bid too high or too low.
- **No risk register.** Surprises.

## Summary

- Contingency: 5-30%+ based on uncertainty.
- Owner + contractor separate buffers.
- Risk register tracks identified hazards.
- Common: weather, materials, subs, site conditions.
- Transfer via insurance, bonds, contracts.
