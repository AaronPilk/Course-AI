# Quantity takeoffs + unit pricing

## What a takeoff is

Count + measure quantities from drawings:
- Concrete: cubic yards.
- Drywall: square feet.
- Framing: linear feet of studs.
- Electrical: outlets, panels, wire feet.
- HVAC: tonnage + duct feet.

For: estimate foundation.

## Manual takeoff

Pre-software:
- Print drawings.
- Highlight/measure with scale.
- Tabulate in spreadsheet.

Tedious; error-prone.

For: legacy method.

## Digital takeoff

Modern tools:
- **Bluebeam Revu.** Most popular for PDF takeoff.
- **PlanSwift.** Easy-use.
- **STACK.** Cloud-based.
- **Procore + integration.** End-to-end.
- **BIM model takeoff.** Quantities from 3D model.

For: efficient takeoffs.

## Unit pricing

For each line item:
- Cost per unit.
- Labor + material + equipment + markup.

Examples:
- Concrete: $150-200/cubic yard placed.
- Drywall: $1.50-3/sq ft installed.
- Outlet: $50-150 installed.

For: pricing math.

## RSMeans

Industry standard cost database:
- 30,000+ line items.
- Updated annually.
- Regional cost factors.

Used by: estimators, owners, court awards.

For: pricing reference.

## Markup factors

Apply to bare costs:
- Overhead: 8-15%.
- Profit: 5-10%.
- Bond: 1-3%.
- Insurance: 2-5%.

Total markup: 15-35%.

For: real pricing.

## Schedule of Values

Breakdown of contract sum by line item:
- Site work.
- Concrete.
- Masonry.
- Framing.
- Roofing.
- Mechanical.
- Electrical.
- Plumbing.
- Finishes.

Used for monthly payment applications.

For: contract structure.

## Cost loading

Sometimes contractors:
- Front-load early items (gets paid faster).
- Cash flow management.

Owners watch for excessive front-loading.

For: cash flow tactics.

## Common estimate types

By accuracy:
- **Conceptual.** ±20-30% (early design).
- **Schematic.** ±15%.
- **Design Development.** ±10%.
- **Construction Documents.** ±5%.
- **Bid.** As tight as you'll get.

For: estimate maturity.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Missing scope.** Underestimated.
- **No waste factor.** Material shortages.
- **Optimistic productivity.** Labor overrun.

## Summary

- Takeoffs: count/measure from drawings.
- Bluebeam most common modern tool.
- Unit prices × quantities + markups = estimate.
- RSMeans for cost database.
- Estimate accuracy improves through design phases.
