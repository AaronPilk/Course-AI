# Stakeholders + RACI

## Typical stakeholders

- **Owner.** Pays; sets goals.
- **Architect.** Designs; quality.
- **Engineer.** Structural, MEP, civil.
- **General Contractor.** Builds.
- **Subcontractors.** Specific trades.
- **Suppliers.** Materials, equipment.
- **Inspectors.** AHJ (Authority Having Jurisdiction).
- **End users.** Tenants, occupants.
- **Neighbors.** Noise, disruption.
- **Lenders.** Construction loans.

For: cast of characters.

## Owner types

- **Owner-occupier.** Will live/work there.
- **Investor.** Selling/leasing post-construction.
- **Public.** Government, school district.
- **Developer.** Multi-property professional.

Different priorities, sophistication.

For: motivation understanding.

## GC vs. Owner-rep vs. CM

- **General Contractor.** Performs work.
- **Owner's Representative.** Hired by owner; oversees on owner's behalf.
- **Construction Manager.** Coordinator; may or may not have construction risk.

For: roles clarity.

## RACI matrix

For each task assign:
- **R (Responsible).** Does the work.
- **A (Accountable).** Final authority (one only).
- **C (Consulted).** Provides input.
- **I (Informed).** Kept in loop.

Example for "Foundation pour":
```
                 Owner  PM  Super  Sub  Inspector
Inspect formwork:  I    A    R     C    R
Schedule pour:     I    A    R     C    -
Pour concrete:     -    A    R     R    I
Cure + protect:    -    A    R     R    -
QA test:           I    A    C     R    R
```

For: clear responsibilities.

## Communication plan

Define:
- Daily reports.
- Weekly OAC (Owner-Architect-Contractor) meetings.
- Subcontractor coordination meetings.
- Safety stand-downs.
- Critical issue escalation.

Frequency + audience + format.

For: structured comm.

## OAC meetings

Standard weekly meeting:
- Owner.
- Architect.
- Contractor.

Agenda:
- Schedule status.
- Cost report.
- Change orders.
- RFIs (Requests for Information).
- Safety.
- Quality issues.
- Decisions needed.

Documented; signed; controls history.

For: project rhythm.

## RFI process

Contractor questions designer:
- Field condition vs. drawings.
- Design clarification.
- Substitution requests.

Typical: 100s-1000s per project.

Response time critical to schedule.

For: communication mechanism.

## Submittals

Contractor proposes:
- Specific products.
- Shop drawings.
- Color samples.
- Mockups.

Architect approves before installation.

Critical: long-lead items submitted early.

For: design intent verification.

## Pay applications

Monthly payment requests:
- Schedule of Values (line items).
- % complete each line.
- Owner/architect verify.
- Lien waivers required.

Process takes 30-60 days from app to payment.

For: cash flow.

## Lien rights

Subcontractor / supplier rights:
- File mechanic's lien if unpaid.
- Cloud title.
- Lien waivers exchanged at each payment.

Critical legal layer.

For: payment security.

## Project closeout RACI

Last 5%:
- Punch list (R contractor, C architect, A owner).
- As-builts (R contractor).
- Operations manuals (R contractor + suppliers).
- Warranties (R contractor).
- Final inspection (R inspector, A owner).

For: closure responsibility.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **No clear decision-maker.** Project drifts.
- **Owner over-managing.** Contractor disempowered.
- **Skipping OAC meetings.** Lose alignment.

## Summary

- Stakeholders: Owner, Architect, Engineer, GC, Subs, Inspectors, Users.
- RACI matrix: Responsible, Accountable (one), Consulted, Informed.
- OAC weekly = rhythm.
- RFI + Submittal = info flow.
- Pay apps + lien waivers = cash flow + legal.
