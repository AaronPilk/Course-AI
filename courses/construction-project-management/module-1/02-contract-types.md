# Contract types

## Lump sum (fixed price)

Contractor commits to fixed price for defined scope.

Pros: predictable cost; owner risk minimized.
Cons: change orders expensive; contractor pads bid.

For: well-defined projects.

## Cost-plus

Contractor paid actual cost + fee:
- Cost-plus-percentage (e.g., 8% of cost).
- Cost-plus-fixed-fee.
- Cost-plus-incentive (savings shared).

For: uncertain scope; trust required.

## GMP (Guaranteed Maximum Price)

Hybrid:
- Cost-plus until ceiling reached.
- Cap on owner exposure.
- Contractor absorbs overruns.
- Underruns may be shared.

Common in CMAR delivery.

For: balance of risk.

## Time + Materials (T&M)

Hourly labor rate + material markup.

For: small repairs; truly unpredictable work.

Risky for large projects (no incentive to be efficient).

## Unit price

Fixed price per unit (cubic yard concrete, linear foot pipe):
- Used in heavy civil.
- Quantities measured + billed.

For: variable-quantity work.

## Design-bid-build (DBB)

Traditional:
1. Owner hires architect.
2. Drawings completed.
3. Bid out.
4. Lowest bidder builds.

Pros: clear roles; competitive.
Cons: adversarial; no contractor input on design.

For: public projects often required.

## Design-build (DB)

Single entity designs + builds:
- Faster delivery.
- Single point of accountability.
- Less contention.

For: owner wants speed.

## CMAR (Construction Manager at Risk)

CM hired during design:
- Pre-construction services.
- GMP at design completion.
- Construction phase as GC.

Modern hybrid.

For: complex projects with collaboration.

## IPD (Integrated Project Delivery)

Risk + reward shared by owner + designer + contractor:
- Lean principles.
- Single contract.
- BIM-integrated.

For: large, complex, repeat-client projects.

## AIA + ConsensusDocs

Standard form contracts:
- AIA: American Institute of Architects.
- ConsensusDocs: industry coalition.
- Public agencies often use own forms.

Customizable but standard.

For: legal baseline.

## Liquidated damages

Pre-agreed daily penalty for late completion:
- $1,000-100,000+ per day depending on project.
- Replaces having to prove damages.

For: schedule enforcement.

## Retention

Owner withholds 5-10% of each payment:
- Released at substantial completion.
- Or half at substantial, half at final.
- Reduces contractor incentive to walk away.

For: payment leverage.

## Common mistakes

- **Lump sum for unclear scope.** Contractor pads bid + many change orders.
- **No defined liquidated damages.** Loose schedule enforcement.
- **Forgetting retention.** Contractor leaves before punch list complete.

## Summary

- Lump sum / Cost-plus / GMP / T&M / Unit price.
- DBB / DB / CMAR / IPD delivery methods.
- AIA standard forms common.
- Liquidated damages + retention = leverage.
- Match contract to project certainty level.
