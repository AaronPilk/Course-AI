# Project delivery methods

## DBB (Design-Bid-Build)

Traditional sequential:
1. Owner hires architect/engineer.
2. Design completed.
3. Bid out to contractors.
4. Low bidder wins.
5. Contractor builds.

Pros:
- Competitive pricing.
- Clear roles.
- Required for many public projects.

Cons:
- Adversarial.
- No contractor input on design.
- Change orders frequent (gaps in docs).
- Longer schedule.

For: well-defined public projects.

## DB (Design-Build)

Single entity designs + builds:
- Architect/contractor partnered.
- Owner has one contract.
- Often GMP after concept.

Pros:
- Faster (overlap design + construction).
- Single accountability.
- Better collaboration.
- Cost certainty earlier.

Cons:
- Less independent oversight.
- Owner has less control.

For: speed-critical or experienced owner.

## CMAR (Construction Manager at Risk)

Hybrid:
1. Owner hires CM during design (consultant fees).
2. CM provides pre-construction services.
3. At design completion, CM gives GMP.
4. CM becomes GC for construction.

Pros:
- Contractor input on design.
- Cost certainty at design end.
- Collaborative.

Cons:
- More expensive than DBB.
- Risk of CM bias toward easy decisions.

For: complex projects with cost certainty needs.

## IPD (Integrated Project Delivery)

Most collaborative:
- Owner, architect, contractor co-sign.
- Risk/reward shared.
- Lean principles.
- BIM-integrated.
- Profit pool from savings.

Pros:
- Maximum collaboration.
- Best for complex.

Cons:
- Complex contract.
- Few law firms experienced.
- Requires sophisticated owner.

For: complex, repeat-client, BIM-mature teams.

## Multi-prime

Owner contracts directly with multiple primes (electrical, mechanical, GC etc.):
- Owner manages coordination.

Rare; high owner involvement.

For: owners with construction expertise.

## EPC (Engineer-Procure-Construct)

Common in industrial/energy:
- Single contractor handles everything.
- Often turnkey (handover keys to owner).
- Used for: power plants, refineries, infrastructure.

For: process facility projects.

## Public vs. private

Public projects often must use:
- Low-bid DBB (taxpayer protection).
- Sometimes Best Value DB or CMAR.
- Specific procurement rules.

Private has flexibility.

For: regulatory constraints.

## Selecting method

Decision factors:
- Schedule criticality.
- Cost certainty need.
- Scope definition.
- Owner expertise.
- Project complexity.
- Regulatory requirements.

For: matching method to need.

## Modern trends

DBB declining:
- 35% of US construction by value (down from 90% in 1990s).
- DB + CMAR growing.
- IPD slow adoption.

For: industry direction.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Wrong method for project type.** Schedule slips, cost overruns.
- **Cheapest bid for complex.** May not deliver.
- **No collaborative methods for complex BIM.** Misses benefits.

## Summary

- DBB: traditional, sequential, adversarial.
- DB: single entity, fast, single accountability.
- CMAR: CM joins early, GMP at design end.
- IPD: shared risk/reward, BIM-integrated.
- Match method to project complexity + cost certainty needs.
