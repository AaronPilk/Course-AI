# Careers + certifications

## Career paths

- **Project Engineer (PE).** Entry; documentation + RFIs + submittals.
- **Project Manager (PM).** Mid-level; manage projects.
- **Senior PM / Director.** Multiple projects.
- **Estimator / Chief Estimator.** Specialized.
- **Superintendent.** Field-focused.
- **General Superintendent.** Multi-site.
- **VP Operations.** Executive.
- **Owner / GC.** Run business.

For: progression.

## Education

Common backgrounds:
- BS Construction Management.
- BS Civil Engineering.
- BS Architecture.
- MBA after technical degree.

Or experience-based: career started in trades, advanced.

For: degree options.

## Certifications

- **PMP (Project Management Professional).** General PM.
- **CCM (Certified Construction Manager).** CMAA.
- **LEED AP.** Green building.
- **OSHA 30.** Safety supervisor.
- **CMIT.** Apprenticeship cert.
- **AC (Assoc Constructor).** AIC.
- **State GC license.** Run a business.

For: credentials.

## Salary 2026

US ranges:
- PE: $60-90k.
- PM: $90-160k.
- Senior PM: $130-200k.
- Director / VP: $180-300k+.
- Superintendent: $80-160k.
- Estimator: $85-150k.

Bonuses common (10-30% of base).

For: compensation.

## Skills in demand

Technical:
- Estimating.
- Scheduling.
- BIM.
- Contracts.
- Safety.

Soft:
- Communication.
- Negotiation.
- Conflict resolution.
- Leadership.
- Decision under pressure.

For: career skills.

## Field vs. office

- **Field (super).** Outside; hands-on; trades; muddy boots.
- **Office (PM).** Computer-based; budgets + schedules.

Both essential; PM career typically rotates through both.

For: career style choice.

## Specializations

- **Healthcare.** Hospitals.
- **Industrial.** Plants, refineries.
- **Civil.** Roads, bridges, infrastructure.
- **High-rise.** Towers.
- **Retail / hospitality.**
- **Residential.** Custom homes, multi-family.
- **Tenant improvement.** Office buildouts.
- **Green building.** LEED, Passive House.

Each has nuances.

For: niche selection.

## Industries hiring

- **Mega contractors.** Turner, AECOM, Skanska, Bechtel, Whiting-Turner.
- **Regional GCs.** Many.
- **Subcontractors.** Often specialized.
- **CM firms.** Boutique advisors.
- **Owner's reps.** Hospital, university CM departments.

For: employer types.

## Women + diversity

Industry historically male-dominated:
- ~12% women.
- Diversity programs growing.
- NAWIC (National Association of Women in Construction).

Slowly changing.

For: cultural awareness.

## Growth areas 2026+

- Infrastructure (US bipartisan infrastructure bill).
- Data centers.
- Healthcare.
- EV manufacturing.
- Renewable energy.
- Housing (chronic shortage).

For: sector demand.

## Construction tech careers

Newer niche:
- ConTech PMs.
- BIM specialists.
- Software for construction (Procore, etc.).
- Robotics + drones.

Premium salaries; growing fast.

For: tech intersection.

## Entrepreneurship

Many construction pros start own:
- Sub-contracting business.
- Small GC.
- Specialty (renovations, design-build).
- Real estate development.

Capital + license + insurance + first project.

For: entrepreneurial path.

## Continuing education

Industry orgs:
- AGC (Associated General Contractors).
- AIA (Architects).
- CMAA (Construction Management Association of America).
- ABC (Associated Builders + Contractors).

Conferences, training, networking.

For: lifelong learning.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Only office or only field.** Limit yourself.
- **No certifications.** Slow advancement.
- **Single specialty.** Vulnerable to downturn.

## Summary

- PE → PM → Senior PM → Director.
- Field vs. office; rotate both.
- PMP / CCM / LEED / OSHA 30 certifications.
- Salary $60-300k+ range.
- Growth: infrastructure, data centers, healthcare, housing.

That's the course. Next: certifications + first PM role.
