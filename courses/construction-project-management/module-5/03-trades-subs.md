# Trades + subcontractor management

## Major trades

- **Site / earthwork.** Excavation, grading.
- **Concrete.** Foundations, slabs.
- **Masonry.** Brick, block, stone.
- **Steel.** Structural fabrication + erection.
- **Carpentry.** Framing, finishes.
- **Roofing.**
- **Mechanical (HVAC).**
- **Electrical.**
- **Plumbing.**
- **Drywall.**
- **Painting.**
- **Flooring.**
- **Glazing.**
- **Specialty.** Fire suppression, elevators, etc.

For: typical scope.

## Subcontractor selection

Evaluate:
- Capacity (manpower, equipment).
- Safety record (EMR + TRIR).
- Financial stability.
- References.
- Schedule history.
- Pricing.

Lowest bid not always best.

For: prequalification.

## Subcontract types

- **Performance-based.** Sub responsible for outcome.
- **Labor only.** GC supplies materials.
- **T&M.** Hourly for unpredictable.
- **Unit price.** Per unit (yard concrete).

Match to scope risk.

For: contract structure.

## Pre-bid meeting

Before bids due:
- Project tour.
- Question clarification.
- Bidder concerns.
- Schedule discussion.

Reduces ambiguity → better bids.

For: bid quality.

## Bid leveling

After bids received:
- Compare apples-to-apples.
- Identify exclusions.
- Clarify assumptions.

Lowest "as-bid" not always lowest after adjustment.

For: fair comparison.

## Subcontract document

Standard contents:
- Scope.
- Schedule milestones.
- Price + payment terms.
- Insurance + bond requirements.
- Performance standards.
- Change order process.
- Dispute resolution.
- Termination.

ConsensusDocs or AIA forms common.

For: legal.

## Pre-construction meeting

With sub before they mobilize:
- Schedule confirmation.
- Site access.
- Safety expectations.
- Submittal schedule.
- Coordination needs.

Sets tone.

For: kickoff.

## Coordination

Daily/weekly with trade super:
- Today's plan.
- Tomorrow's plan.
- Blockers.
- Material status.
- Manpower.

Sometimes pull planning sessions.

For: rhythm.

## Trade super relationship

- Treat as partner.
- Listen to field input.
- Solve problems together.
- Respect their expertise.

GC PM/super shapes culture.

For: relationship management.

## Payment + retention

- Pay on time per contract.
- Retain 5-10% until substantial completion.
- Release retention promptly.
- Pay subs before contractors get paid (some contracts).

Late payment = sub leaves project.

For: cash flow management.

## Quality control

- Inspections per spec.
- Submittals reviewed.
- Mockups for finish trades.
- Punch list at end of each trade.

Each trade signs off on their work.

For: quality assurance.

## Schedule enforcement

- Liquidated damages from sub.
- Backcharge if extra work caused by sub.
- Default + replace (last resort).

For: schedule risk.

## Sub safety

- GC responsible for site safety.
- Subs follow GC site safety plan.
- Sub safety reports + meetings.
- Sub PPE compliance.

OSHA citations to GC.

For: shared safety.

## Common conflicts

- Schedule disputes.
- Material availability.
- Scope creep.
- Quality issues.
- Payment disputes.

Resolution:
- Project manager.
- VP/Owner escalation.
- Mediation.
- Arbitration.

For: dispute handling.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Lowest bid blind.** Lower quality / drama.
- **Late pay.** Sub leaves.
- **No quality verification.** Punch list balloon.

## Summary

- 14+ major trades on typical project.
- Prequalify subs on safety + capacity + finances.
- Subcontract document + pre-con meeting.
- Daily coordination + pull planning.
- Pay timely; retention 5-10%.
