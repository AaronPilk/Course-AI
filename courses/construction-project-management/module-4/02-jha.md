# Job Hazard Analysis (JHA)

## What JHA is

For each task:
1. List steps.
2. Identify hazards per step.
3. Define controls per hazard.

Document signed by crew.

Also called AHA (Activity Hazard Analysis), JSA (Job Safety Analysis).

For: pre-task safety planning.

## When required

Before high-risk work:
- New crew.
- New procedure.
- Daily for changing conditions.

Many projects: JHA for every task daily.

For: discipline.

## JHA template

```
Activity: Install rooftop HVAC unit

Step 1: Move equipment onto roof
  Hazards: Fall from height; struck-by from crane
  Controls: Tie-off PFAS; clear exclusion zone; spotter

Step 2: Connect electrical
  Hazards: Electrocution
  Controls: LOTO; verify de-energized; insulated tools

Step 3: Final connections
  Hazards: Refrigerant exposure; sharp metal
  Controls: PPE; gloves; eye protection
```

For: structured analysis.

## Hierarchy of controls

Most effective to least:
1. **Elimination.** Remove hazard entirely.
2. **Substitution.** Replace with safer.
3. **Engineering controls.** Physical barriers, ventilation.
4. **Administrative controls.** Training, procedures.
5. **PPE.** Last line of defense.

Engineering > admin > PPE.

For: control selection.

## Pre-task safety meeting

Daily morning toolbox talk:
- Day's work.
- JHA review.
- New hazards.
- Q&A.

15-20 minutes; required by most contractors.

For: rhythm.

## STOP work authority

Any worker can stop work for safety concern:
- Without fear of retaliation.
- Required by modern programs.

Empowerment = better safety culture.

For: empowerment.

## Near-miss reporting

Capture incidents that almost caused injury:
- Less stigma than injury report.
- Trend analysis.
- Predictive of future incidents.

Target: 10× near-misses per injury reported.

For: leading indicator.

## Risk assessment matrix

Score each hazard:
- Severity (1-5).
- Probability (1-5).
- Risk = S × P.

Examples:
- 1-4: Low. Standard controls.
- 5-9: Medium. Engineering controls.
- 10-15: High. Stop + review.
- 16-25: Extreme. Re-plan task.

For: prioritization.

## Confined spaces

Permit-required:
- Tanks, vaults, sewers, pits.
- Atmosphere test before entry.
- Standby person.
- Rescue plan.
- Permit signed.

OSHA-regulated; deaths common when ignored.

For: special category.

## Hot work permits

Welding, cutting, grinding:
- Fire watch.
- Fire extinguisher at site.
- Permit signed.

For: spark-generating work.

## Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)

For energized equipment:
- Isolate energy.
- Lock + tag with worker's identifier.
- Verify zero energy.
- Only worker who locked can remove.

Critical for electricians, mechanics.

For: energy control.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Generic JHA.** Doesn't address actual hazards.
- **Pencil-whipping.** Signed but not discussed.
- **No update for new conditions.** Stale plan.

## Summary

- JHA = task-level hazard plan.
- Steps → hazards → controls per step.
- Hierarchy: Eliminate > Substitute > Engineering > Admin > PPE.
- Pre-task meetings + near-miss reporting.
- LOTO, hot work permits, confined space permits.
