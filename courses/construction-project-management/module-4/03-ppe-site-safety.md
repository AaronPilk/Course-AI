# PPE + site safety planning

## Standard PPE

Required on most construction sites:
- **Hard hat.** ANSI Z89.1 Class C/E/G.
- **Safety glasses.** ANSI Z87.1.
- **Steel-toe boots.** ASTM F2413.
- **High-visibility vest.** ANSI 107 Class 2.
- **Gloves.** Task-appropriate.
- **Hearing protection.** When >85 dB.

For: minimum baseline.

## Task-specific PPE

Beyond baseline:
- **Respirators.** Silica, paint fumes.
- **Welding helmets.** UV protection.
- **Cut-resistant gloves.** Glass, metal handling.
- **Fall protection harness.** Heights.
- **Face shields.** Grinding, splash.
- **Insulated gloves.** Electrical work.

For: hazard-specific.

## PPE limitations

- Last line of defense (after elimination/engineering).
- Must fit properly.
- Must be inspected.
- Must be used correctly.

For: realistic.

## Site safety plan

Document covering:
- Hazard analysis.
- Emergency procedures.
- Evacuation routes.
- Medical response.
- Fire response.
- Severe weather.
- First aid stations.
- Specific hazards.

Project-specific; required for many.

For: comprehensive prep.

## Emergency action plan

- Evacuation routes posted.
- Assembly points.
- Account for all personnel.
- Emergency contacts.
- First aid + AED locations.
- Hospital info + route.

Drilled periodically.

For: emergency response.

## Site security

- Fencing perimeter.
- Signage (no trespass, hard hat area).
- Locked gates after hours.
- Material lockup.
- Cameras.

Prevents theft + liability.

For: site protection.

## Public protection

- Sidewalk closures or covered walkways.
- Barricades.
- Traffic control.
- Dust + noise mitigation.
- Vibration monitoring (adjacent structures).

Required for urban work.

For: neighbors + public.

## Crane safety

- Annual inspections (OSHA).
- Operator certifications (CCO).
- Daily pre-shift checks.
- Pre-lift plans.
- Exclusion zones.
- Signal person.

Crane incidents catastrophic; many rules.

For: high-risk equipment.

## Scaffolding

OSHA 1926 Subpart L:
- Designed by qualified person.
- Inspected daily.
- Capacity 4× max load.
- Guardrails 38-45" high.

Erected by trained crew.

For: working platforms.

## Trenching

Major fatality category:
- Sloping / shoring / shielding at 5+ ft.
- Inspect daily by competent person.
- Egress within 25 ft.
- Spoils 2 ft from edge.

Trench collapse buries victim under tons of soil in seconds.

For: critical safety.

## Toolbox talks

Daily/weekly 10-15 min safety topics:
- Topic of the week.
- Recent incidents.
- Sharing experiences.

Required by most contractors.

For: ongoing engagement.

## Drug + alcohol testing

Required:
- Pre-employment.
- Post-incident.
- Random.
- Reasonable suspicion.

DOT-regulated for CDL drivers.

For: substance-free workplace.

## Subcontractor management

GC responsible for sub safety:
- Prequalify safety records.
- Require safety plans.
- Audit on site.
- Hold sub safety meetings.

For: shared responsibility.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **PPE only without engineering.** Incomplete.
- **No site security.** Theft + injury.
- **Skipping crane inspections.** Catastrophic risk.

## Summary

- PPE baseline + task-specific.
- Site safety plan + emergency procedures.
- Crane + scaffold + trenching = critical equipment areas.
- Toolbox talks daily.
- Sub safety management = GC responsibility.
