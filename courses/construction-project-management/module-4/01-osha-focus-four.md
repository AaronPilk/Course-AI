# OSHA 30 + Focus Four hazards

## OSHA

Occupational Safety and Health Administration (1970):
- Sets workplace safety standards.
- Conducts inspections.
- Issues citations + fines.
- 29 CFR Part 1926 = construction-specific rules.

For: federal framework.

## OSHA 10 + OSHA 30

Training certifications:
- **OSHA 10.** 10 hours; entry workers; covers basics.
- **OSHA 30.** 30 hours; supervisors; deeper coverage.

Many states + projects require:
- Workers: OSHA 10.
- Supervisors: OSHA 30.

Online courses ~$50-150.

For: required training.

## Focus Four hazards

OSHA's 4 leading construction killer categories:
1. **Falls.** #1 cause; ~33% of fatalities.
2. **Struck-by.** Vehicles, falling objects.
3. **Caught-in/between.** Trenches, machinery.
4. **Electrocution.** Power lines, tools, wires.

For: priority hazards.

## Fall protection

OSHA: required at 6+ feet (general construction):
- **Guardrails.** Top rail 42" + midrail.
- **Personal Fall Arrest System (PFAS).** Harness + lanyard + anchor.
- **Safety nets.** Below work area.
- **Hole covers.** Marked + secured.

Falls cause 1/3 of all construction deaths.

For: most critical.

## Struck-by hazards

Sources:
- Backing vehicles (use spotter).
- Falling tools (toeboards, lanyards).
- Cranes / hoisting.
- Saw blades, nail guns.

Mitigations: PPE, traffic patterns, exclusion zones.

For: vehicle + tool risks.

## Caught-in/between

- Trenches collapse (require sloping/shoring at 5+ ft).
- Machinery moving parts.
- Equipment pinch points.

Trench deaths typically: collapsing soil 1 cubic yard = 1 ton.

For: confined space risk.

## Electrocution

- Overhead power lines (maintain 10+ ft clearance for <50kV).
- Damaged tools.
- Wet conditions.
- Improper grounding.

Lockout/Tagout for energized equipment.

For: electrical safety.

## OSHA inspections

Triggered by:
- Imminent danger.
- Fatality / catastrophe.
- Worker complaint.
- Targeted programs.
- Random.

Penalties:
- Willful: up to $156k.
- Serious: up to $15k.
- Repeat: up to $156k.

Per violation.

For: enforcement.

## Recordable injuries

OSHA 300 log entries:
- Days away from work.
- Restricted work.
- Medical treatment beyond first aid.
- Loss of consciousness.

DART rate metric: Days Away, Restricted, Transferred.

For: tracking.

## TRIR (Total Recordable Incident Rate)

```
TRIR = (recordable incidents × 200,000) / total hours worked
```

Industry average ~3.0. Best contractors <1.0.

OSHA + insurance + clients track.

For: safety benchmark.

## Heat illness

Increasing OSHA focus:
- Heat-related deaths rising.
- Provide water, rest, shade.
- Acclimatization required.
- Some states (CA) mandate.

For: emerging compliance.

## Silica + asbestos

- Crystalline silica (concrete dust): OSHA silica standard 2017.
- Asbestos (older buildings): always managed.
- Respirators + wet methods.

For: respiratory health.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **No OSHA 10/30 cards on site.** Inspector violation.
- **No fall protection >6 ft.** #1 violation cited.
- **Untrained operators.** Equipment violations.

## Summary

- OSHA 1926 = construction safety rules.
- OSHA 10 (workers) + OSHA 30 (supervisors).
- Focus Four: Falls, Struck-by, Caught-in, Electrocution.
- Fall protection required 6+ ft.
- TRIR < 1.0 is excellent.
