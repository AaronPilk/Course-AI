# Incident reporting + investigation

## Reporting requirements

To OSHA within 8 hours:
- Fatality.

Within 24 hours:
- Hospitalizations.
- Amputations.
- Loss of an eye.

Phone 1-800-321-OSHA or online form.

For: federal requirements.

## Internal reporting

For all incidents:
- Injury reports.
- Near-misses.
- Property damage.
- Environmental releases.

Document within 24 hours.

For: company tracking.

## Initial response

When incident occurs:
1. Stop work.
2. Medical aid.
3. Secure scene.
4. Notify supervision.
5. Begin investigation.
6. Notify owner + insurance.
7. Possible OSHA report.

For: standard response.

## Scene preservation

For serious incidents:
- Don't disturb equipment.
- Photograph extensively.
- Preserve evidence.
- Mark positions of materials/tools.

OSHA inspector will come.

For: investigation integrity.

## Witness interviews

Within 24 hours:
- Separate witnesses.
- Open-ended questions.
- Don't lead.
- Record statements.
- Get signed.

Stories drift over time.

For: factual basis.

## Root cause analysis

Beyond "what happened":
- **5 Whys.** Ask "why" 5 times to drill to root.
- **Fishbone diagram.** Cause + effect.
- **Bow-tie analysis.** Threats + barriers.

Avoid blame; focus on systems.

For: structured analysis.

## Common root causes

- Inadequate training.
- Poor planning.
- Equipment failure.
- Unsafe procedures.
- Inadequate supervision.
- Pressure to rush.
- Lack of resources.

Rarely single cause.

For: pattern recognition.

## Corrective actions

For each root cause:
- Specific action.
- Owner.
- Due date.
- Verification.

Track to completion.

For: closing loop.

## OSHA inspection

If they come:
- Polite + cooperative.
- Walk-through.
- Document review.
- Worker interviews.
- Closing conference.
- Citations within 6 months.

Right to contest.

For: dealing with OSHA.

## Citation categories

- **Willful.** Up to $156,259.
- **Serious.** Up to $15,625.
- **Other-than-serious.** Up to $15,625.
- **Repeat.** Up to $156,259.
- **Failure to abate.** $15,625/day.

For: penalties.

## EMR (Experience Modification Rate)

Workers compensation insurance multiplier:
- 1.00 = industry average.
- < 1.00 = better than average; lower premiums.
- > 1.00 = worse; higher premiums.

Many owners require <1.0 to bid.

For: business impact.

## Insurance + workers comp

- Workers comp pays for injured workers' medical + lost wages.
- Employers pay premiums based on EMR + payroll.
- Catastrophic incidents can trigger insurance review.

For: financial.

## Lessons learned

Share across organization:
- Newsletters.
- Toolbox talks.
- Database.
- Incident database.

Don't let incidents repeat.

For: continuous improvement.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Not reporting.** Federal violation.
- **Blame individuals.** Misses systemic issues.
- **No corrective action.** Recurs.
- **Adversarial with OSHA.** Worse outcomes.

## Summary

- OSHA: 8-hour fatality, 24-hour hospitalization.
- Document all incidents + near-misses.
- Root cause analysis (5 Whys, Fishbone).
- Corrective actions tracked.
- EMR drives insurance + bid eligibility.
