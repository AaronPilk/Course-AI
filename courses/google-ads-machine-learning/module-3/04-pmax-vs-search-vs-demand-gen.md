# PMax vs Search vs Demand Gen

## The three workhorses

In 2026, three campaign types do most of the work for performance accounts:

- **Search** — text ads on Google search results pages. Highest intent, oldest format.
- **Performance Max** — cross-surface automation across all Google properties.
- **Demand Gen** — visual ads on YouTube, Discover, Gmail, optimized for awareness-to-consideration.

Display Network and traditional Shopping campaigns still exist but have largely been absorbed into PMax for most accounts. Display gets used standalone mainly for retargeting or specialized brand campaigns.

Choosing the right mix of Search + PMax + Demand Gen is one of the bigger strategic decisions for any account.

## Search campaigns — what they're for

Search remains the highest-intent surface on Google. Users type a query expressing a specific need; ads appear in response. Conversion intent is generally the highest of any ad surface.

Best for:

- **Brand defense.** Bid on your brand keywords on exact match in a separate campaign. Always.
- **High-intent commercial keywords.** "Buy [product] online," "[service] near me," "best [category]."
- **Competitor brand bidding** (where legally appropriate).
- **Categories where users actively search.** B2B SaaS, services, lead-gen products.
- **Granular control needs.** When you want to see specific keyword performance, control match types, set keyword-level negatives.

Drawbacks:

- Limited to Google Search surface (and Search Partners if you enable them, which most accounts shouldn't).
- Misses cross-surface customer journeys.
- Less efficient on top-of-funnel where intent is still forming.

## Performance Max — what it's for

PMax is for cross-surface acquisition. Best for:

- **E-commerce with product feeds.** Big PMax sweet spot.
- **Multi-surface customer journeys.** Display + YouTube + Search + Discover all play roles.
- **Mass-market DTC.** Broad audiences, strong creative variety.
- **Accounts with rich first-party data.** Customer Match-driven lookalike acquisition.
- **Operations that want full automation.** Less manual labor in exchange for less granular control.

Drawbacks:

- Reduced reporting visibility.
- Less control over specific placements or audiences.
- Can absorb brand queries that should go to dedicated brand Search.
- Performs poorly on thin signal or thin creative.

## Demand Gen — what it's for

Demand Gen is Google's answer to TikTok/Reels-style discovery ads. It serves on YouTube (Shorts, in-stream, in-feed), Discover, and Gmail. Optimized for top-of-funnel discovery and consideration.

Best for:

- **Brand awareness.** Reaching users not yet searching for your category.
- **Product launches.** Driving consideration for new products.
- **Visual-led categories.** Fashion, beauty, home goods, food.
- **Video creative.** Vertical short-form video is the native format.
- **Lookalike-style acquisition.** Using customer match for lookalike audiences explicitly.

Drawbacks:

- Lower intent than Search; CPA tends to be higher per direct conversion.
- View-through attribution can mislead.
- Different optimization rhythm than Search/PMax.

Demand Gen is fairly new in its current form (rebranded from Discovery Ads in 2024); rapidly maturing.

## When campaigns overlap

The biggest overlap risk: Search + PMax bidding on the same queries.

Google's documented rule: **exact-match Search keywords take priority over PMax for matching that exact query.** Otherwise, both can match. In practice:

- Brand Search campaigns with exact-match brand keywords catch brand queries before PMax.
- Non-brand exact-match keywords catch their queries.
- Phrase and broad match keywords don't have the same priority over PMax — both can serve.
- PMax can match queries you don't have keywords for.

The implication: brand Search exact-match is mandatory; otherwise PMax will absorb brand queries with inferior handling.

For non-brand Search vs PMax, you'll see some overlap. Reasonable practice:

- Run a non-brand Search campaign for specific high-intent keywords you want explicit control over.
- Let PMax handle the broader keyword universe.
- Accept some overlap; monitor for cannibalization (Search impressions dropping where PMax serves) and adjust.

## When PMax replaces Search

A controversial position: for some e-commerce accounts, PMax can fully replace non-brand Search. Why:

- PMax's Search-surface serving is sometimes as effective as dedicated Search.
- Operating one campaign is cheaper than two.
- PMax's cross-surface coordination compounds.

Counterargument: dedicated Search lets you see and control specifically what's working. The visibility loss with PMax-only is real.

For most accounts, the balance is: brand Search exact-match (always) + 1-2 non-brand Search campaigns for top-intent keywords + PMax for the rest. Not pure PMax.

## When PMax loses to dedicated Search

Some categories: Search outperforms PMax cleanly.

- **B2B with narrow audience.** PMax's broad-by-design hurts narrow B2B; Search lets you target specific commercial keywords directly.
- **Legal, medical, regulated.** PMax's surface mixing creates compliance issues; dedicated Search with tight match and negatives is safer.
- **Local services with specific geo and keyword intent.** "Plumber Brooklyn 11215" is a Search problem, not a PMax one.

## Demand Gen and the brand awareness problem

Most performance accounts under-invest in top-of-funnel brand demand. Reasons:

- Top-of-funnel CPAs are higher; they look worse in Smart Bidding reports.
- Attribution often misses brand-awareness contribution (view-through, longer cycles).
- It's easier to scale acquisition with proven channels than to invest in awareness.

But neglecting awareness eventually caps performance. Without new entrants into the consideration funnel, performance acquisition saturates.

Demand Gen is the modern tool for this. Allocate 10-20% of paid budget to Demand Gen for awareness/consideration. Measure with brand-search lift, direct traffic increases, and longer-window attribution.

## A typical mix

For a $50k/month e-commerce account:

- **Brand Search (exact)**: 10% of budget. CPA tightest. Guarantees brand defense.
- **Non-brand Search (high-intent keywords)**: 15-20%. CPA mid-tight. Specific keyword control.
- **Performance Max**: 50-60%. CPA on target. Main acquisition engine.
- **Demand Gen**: 10-15%. CPA looser. Awareness fuel for the funnel.
- **Retargeting (Display or Demand Gen)**: 5-10%. CPA tight but lower-incrementality.

Adjust to fit. Smaller accounts might skip Demand Gen until budget supports it.

## Don't forget about App Campaigns

For mobile app businesses, App Campaigns are a separate beast. Similar in spirit to PMax (automation across surfaces) but specifically for app install optimization.

App Campaigns won't be covered in depth here; if your business is an app, study App Campaigns specifically.

## Cannibalization detection

Common cannibalization signs:

- Search impression share drops when PMax is launched.
- Branded organic clicks drop (PMax stealing branded clicks because brand Search exact-match isn't tight enough).
- Total revenue holds but Search attributed conversions decline (PMax claiming what Search would have).

Tools to detect:

- Auction insights reports.
- Brand search impression share tracking.
- Incrementality tests (covered Module 4) — pause one campaign type and see if total revenue holds.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **No separate brand Search campaign.** PMax absorbs brand queries badly.
- **All-PMax strategy with no Search.** Loses granular control for specific high-intent keywords.
- **Skipping Demand Gen.** Top-of-funnel atrophy eventually caps performance.
- **Running both PMax and old Shopping campaigns on same products.** Self-cannibalization.
- **Search Partners enabled by default.** Usually adds low-quality traffic.

## Summary

- Search: highest intent, brand defense, specific control.
- PMax: cross-surface automation, e-commerce strength.
- Demand Gen: top-of-funnel visual discovery.
- Always run brand Search exact-match as separate campaign.
- Reasonable mix: brand Search + non-brand Search + PMax + Demand Gen.
- Monitor for cannibalization; reset structure if Search loses to PMax on same queries.
