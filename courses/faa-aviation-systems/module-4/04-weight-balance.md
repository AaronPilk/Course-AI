# Weight + balance + performance

## Why W+B matters

Out-of-limits aircraft:
- Hard to control.
- Stalls earlier.
- Loses lift faster.
- Stress airframe.

Within envelope = handles as designed.

For: safety + legality.

## Key terms

- **Empty weight.** Aircraft with oil + unusable fuel.
- **Useful load.** Max gross - empty.
- **Max gross weight (MGW).** Most aircraft can weigh.
- **CG (Center of Gravity).** Horizontal balance point.
- **Arm.** Distance from datum (reference point).
- **Moment.** Weight × arm.

For: terminology.

## Calculation

```
Moment = Weight × Arm
Total Moment / Total Weight = CG location
```

Example:
- Empty: 1,500 lb @ 36" arm = 54,000 in-lb
- Pilot: 170 @ 38 = 6,460
- Pax: 150 @ 38 = 5,700
- Fuel: 120 @ 40 = 4,800
- Total: 1,940 lb / 70,960 in-lb / 36.6"

CG = 36.6". Check vs. envelope.

For: math.

## CG envelope

POH shows allowed CG range vs. weight as graphical envelope.

Forward CG: more stable, harder to flare.
Aft CG: less stable, easier to stall.

Both ends limited.

For: safe operation.

## Practical limits

Common Cessna 172:
- MGW: 2,450 lb.
- Empty: ~1,650 lb.
- Useful: 800 lb.
- 4 average adults + full fuel exceeds = leave fuel or pax behind.

For: real-world impact.

## Takeoff performance

POH gives takeoff distance vs.:
- Weight.
- Pressure altitude.
- Temperature.
- Wind.
- Surface (grass +20%).

Add 50% safety margin to charts.

For: required runway.

## Landing performance

Similar charts:
- Distance varies with weight, density altitude, wind.
- Add safety margin.
- Plan for max landing weight (always within limits).

For: arrival planning.

## Climb performance

Service ceiling: where rate of climb drops to 100 fpm.

GA piston aircraft: ~10-12k ft service ceiling. Above: oxygen required (in many jurisdictions).

For: altitude limits.

## Hand-fly capability

Higher loaded aircraft:
- Greater stall speed.
- Less maneuverability.
- More speed to fly approaches.

Plan flight + arrival accordingly.

For: technique adjustment.

## Common mistakes

- **Average passenger weight.** Use actual; ATL airlines use 200 lb male typical now.
- **Baggage in nose vs. tail.** Affects CG.
- **Full fuel = full tank assumption.** Always confirm fuel level.

For: data accuracy.

## Performance vs. weight tradeoff

Reduce weight if performance marginal:
- Less fuel + plan fuel stop.
- Leave passenger.
- Skip baggage.

For: safe planning.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Over MGW.** Illegal + dangerous.
- **CG out of envelope.** Loss of control.
- **Ignoring DA.** Takeoff roll exceeds runway.

## Summary

- W+B: every flight; CG within envelope.
- Performance: takeoff + landing distance vs. conditions.
- Service ceiling limits altitude.
- Heavy aircraft = more runway + less climb.
- Conservative margins on POH numbers.
