# Flight planning fundamentals

## Pre-flight planning steps

Required by FAR 91.103:
1. Weather (METAR, TAF, PIREP, SIGMET).
2. Fuel requirements.
3. Alternates available.
4. Aircraft performance.
5. Runway lengths.
6. NOTAMs + TFRs.
7. Traffic delays.

For: legal compliance.

## Route planning

Tools:
- **ForeFlight.** Route planner with terrain.
- **SkyVector.** Free online.
- **FltPlan Go.** Free alternative.
- **Paper.** Sectional + plotter.

Plot direct or via airways/waypoints.

For: route selection.

## Fuel requirements

FAR 91.151 (VFR):
- Day: fuel for destination + 30 min reserve.
- Night: 45 min reserve.

FAR 91.167 (IFR):
- Destination.
- Alternate (when required).
- Plus 45 min cruise reserve.

For: legal fuel.

## Alternate required?

IFR:
- If destination forecast at ETA-1 to ETA+1 has ceiling <2,000 ft OR visibility <3 SM:
- Alternate required.

Alternate must meet weather mins (varies by approach type available).

For: planning safety.

## Weight + balance

Calculate before each flight:
- Empty weight + arm.
- Each load + arm.
- Sum of moments.
- Center of gravity within envelope.

Out-of-envelope = handling problems, illegal.

For: aircraft safety.

## Performance calculations

For each flight:
- Takeoff distance (density altitude affected).
- Climb performance.
- Cruise fuel burn.
- Landing distance.

Use POH (Pilot's Operating Handbook) charts.

For: realistic capabilities.

## Density altitude

Air density depends on:
- Altitude.
- Temperature.
- Pressure.
- Humidity.

Hot day + high field = high DA = poor performance.

Critical for mountain flying.

For: hazard awareness.

## Cross-country planning

Cross-country = 50+ NM from departure (for time logging).

Standard plan:
- Departure.
- Climb to cruise.
- Cruise checkpoints every 10-20 NM.
- Descent + approach.
- Landing.

Each checkpoint: heading, altitude, time, fuel.

For: navigation log.

## Filing flight plan

Required for IFR; optional for VFR.

```
1. ICAO type (Y, V, I).
2. Aircraft ID.
3. Aircraft type.
4. Wake category (L, M, H).
5. Equipment.
6. Departure airport + time.
7. Cruising speed + altitude.
8. Route.
9. Destination + ETE.
10. Alternate.
11. Pilot info.
12. Fuel remaining.
13. Passengers.
14. Color of aircraft.
```

ForeFlight makes easy.

For: routine ops.

## Opening + closing VFR flight plan

VFR plan:
- Active when contact ATC/FSS.
- Close upon landing (or auto-close in ForeFlight).
- Not closed = SAR (Search And Rescue) initiated.

For: safety net.

## Routing types

- **Direct.** GPS direct waypoint.
- **Airway.** V-routes (low) or J-routes (high).
- **STAR.** Standard Terminal Arrival Route.
- **SID.** Standard Instrument Departure.

ATC may amend.

For: route options.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **No alternate when required.** Illegal IFR.
- **No closed flight plan.** SAR scrambled needlessly.
- **Wrong density altitude.** Performance shortfall.

## Summary

- Pre-flight planning required by 91.103.
- Fuel reserves: 30 min day VFR, 45 min night/IFR.
- Alternate required if destination weather marginal.
- Weight + balance + performance per flight.
- Density altitude critical mountain flying.
- File + close flight plans.
