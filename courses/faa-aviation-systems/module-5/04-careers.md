# Careers in aviation

## Pilot career paths

- **Airline (Part 121).** Highest paid; bureaucratic.
- **Charter (Part 135).** On-demand; less predictable.
- **Corporate.** Private jets; varied.
- **Cargo.** UPS, FedEx; lifestyle different.
- **Military.** Train then transition.
- **Flight instructor.** Build hours.
- **Tour / banner.** Niche.
- **EMS / firefighting.** Mission-driven.

For: varied options.

## Airline progression

Regional → Major:
- **Regional.** SkyWest, Republic, Mesa. $60-100k pilot pay.
- **National.** Spirit, Frontier. $100-200k.
- **Major.** Delta, United, American. $150-400k+ (senior captain).

5-15 years to major.

For: progression.

## Pilot demand 2026

Boeing projects 600,000+ new pilots needed globally by 2042. US shortage of regional pilots particularly.

Why:
- Retirements (mandatory 65 age).
- Travel growth.
- New routes / aircraft.

For: market context.

## ATC controller

Federal job:
- Highly competitive entry.
- $40k-180k+.
- Mandatory retirement at 56.
- Excellent pension.

Stressful but rewarding.

For: government track.

## Aerospace engineering

- Boeing, Airbus, Lockheed, SpaceX, Blue Origin.
- $90-200k+.
- Design + manufacture.
- Various specializations.

For: technical track.

## Aviation maintenance (A&P)

Airframe + Powerplant mechanic:
- 18 months school + cert.
- $50-100k.
- Hands-on.
- Always in demand.

For: skilled trades.

## Avionics tech

Electronics in aircraft:
- Specialized A&P avionics.
- $60-120k.
- Modern aircraft = more avionics.

For: electronics path.

## Pilot vs. engineer salaries

Long-term:
- Captain at major: $400k+.
- Senior aero engineer: $250k+.
- Both excellent careers.

Captain: longer training + more risk.
Engineer: more flexible, fewer hours.

For: lifestyle compare.

## Drone careers

Growing field:
- Real estate photography.
- Construction inspection.
- Survey + mapping.
- $50-150k freelance / employed.
- Part 107 certification only ~$200.

Low barrier to entry.

For: drone specifically.

## Aerospace AI / autonomy

Emerging:
- Autonomous flight (self-driving aircraft).
- AI in air traffic management.
- Predictive maintenance.
- $130-300k engineers.

Fast-growing area.

For: AI intersection.

## Where to start

Options:
- **Discovery flight.** $100 lesson.
- **Air force / Navy ROTC.** Military path.
- **University aviation program.** ATP-CTP eligible.
- **Part 61 flight school.** Most flexible.
- **Part 141 flight school.** Lower hour requirements.

For: getting started.

## Financial reality

Total cost (zero to airline pilot):
- $80-120k+ at flight schools.
- Less if military path.
- Various financing + loans.

Salary recovery: ~5-7 years post-major-hire.

For: investment + return.

## Geography

Best aviation jobs:
- US: Atlanta (Delta), Dallas (American), Memphis (FedEx), Seattle (Boeing).
- Worldwide: Singapore (Asia hub), Dubai (Emirates), Houston (energy).

For: location.

## Soft skills matter

Beyond technical:
- Communication.
- Decision under pressure.
- Crew Resource Management.
- Customer service (airline).
- Leadership.

For: career success.

## Industry future

Major shifts:
- Sustainable aviation fuel mandates.
- Hybrid + electric regional aircraft.
- Pilot shortage = better wages.
- Autonomous cargo eventually.
- More commercial space travel.

For: where heading.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Underestimating training cost.** Plan financing.
- **Single career focus.** Have backup.
- **Ignoring health.** Class 1 medical critical.

## Summary

- Pilot: regional ($60k) → major captain ($400k).
- ATC ($40-180k), engineering ($90-200k), A&P ($50-100k).
- Drone career ~$200 entry; large freelance market.
- Major shortage of pilots globally.
- Mandatory retirement at 65 (pilot), 56 (ATC).

That's the course. Next: discovery flight, decide path, invest in training.
