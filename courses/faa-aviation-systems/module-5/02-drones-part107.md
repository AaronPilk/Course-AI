# Drones (Part 107)

## Part 107

FAA's Small UAS rule (since 2016):
- Commercial drone operations.
- < 55 lbs.
- Remote Pilot Certificate required.

For: commercial drone basics.

## Remote Pilot Certificate

Get via:
- 16 years old.
- Pass 60-question knowledge test (FAA exam).
- TSA security check.
- $175 + study time.

Renew every 24 months (free online).

For: certification.

## Part 107 rules

- Daytime + civil twilight (with anti-collision lights).
- Below 400 ft AGL.
- Stay within visual line of sight.
- Yield to manned aircraft.
- One drone per pilot.
- No flying over people (with exceptions).
- 100 mph max ground speed.
- Class B/C/D/surface E: ATC authorization (LAANC).

For: operational limits.

## LAANC

Low Altitude Authorization Notification Capability:
- Automated airspace authorization.
- Apps: Aloft, B4UFLY.
- Near-instant in most airspace.
- Required for controlled airspace under 400 ft.

For: controlled airspace.

## Part 107 vs. recreational

- **Part 107.** Commercial, requires cert.
- **Recreational.** Hobby only, must follow community guidelines (e.g., AMA).

Recreational pilots: TRUST test (free online), under 250 g exempt from registration.

For: hobbyist vs. commercial.

## Drone registration

All drones >250 g:
- Register with FAA.
- $5 for 3 years.
- Display registration number on aircraft.

For: identification.

## Remote ID

Since 2024:
- All drones >250 g must broadcast Remote ID.
- Position + ID over radio.
- Authorities can identify operator.
- Built into newer drones; older need module.

For: drone identification.

## Waivers

Beyond standard Part 107:
- BVLOS (Beyond Visual Line of Sight).
- Night.
- Multiple drones.
- Over people.

Granted with risk mitigation plans.

For: expanded operations.

## Commercial drone use cases

- Real estate photography.
- Construction surveying.
- Agriculture monitoring.
- Inspection (towers, pipelines).
- Delivery (limited).
- Cinematography.
- Public safety.

For: market.

## Industry growth

US drone market 2026: ~$50B+.

Major players: DJI (China), Skydio, Parrot, Autel, AgEagle.

For: scale.

## Drone airspace classes

Roughly:
- 0-200 ft: General drone area.
- 200-400 ft: Part 107 ceiling typically.
- 400+ ft: Manned aviation (with exceptions).

UTM (Unmanned Traffic Management) emerging for coordination.

For: vertical layering.

## TFR violation

Drones particularly susceptible to TFR violations:
- Sports events.
- Fires.
- VIP movements.

Penalties:
- $1,000-32,000 fines.
- Aircraft seizure.

For: legal risk.

## Counter-drone

Anti-drone tech growing:
- RF jammers.
- Net guns.
- Hawk training (yes, real).
- Drone-on-drone interception.

Civilian counter-drone limited; gov can disable.

For: emerging tech.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Flying near airports without LAANC.** Federal violation.
- **No registration.** Mandatory.
- **No Remote ID.** Violation since 2024.
- **Hobby-then-paid.** Cross to Part 107 required.

## Summary

- Part 107 for commercial drones.
- Remote Pilot Cert via FAA exam.
- LAANC for controlled airspace.
- Remote ID required since 2024.
- 400 ft ceiling, visual line of sight, daytime+.
- BVLOS via waiver.
