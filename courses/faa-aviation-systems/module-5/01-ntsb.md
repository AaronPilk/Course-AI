# NTSB + incident investigation

## NTSB

National Transportation Safety Board:
- Independent of FAA.
- Investigates all civil aviation accidents.
- Issues safety recommendations (no enforcement power).
- Publishes reports (probable cause, contributing factors).

For: institutional setup.

## Accident vs. incident

- **Accident.** Death, serious injury, substantial aircraft damage.
- **Incident.** Less severe events; may still require investigation.

Both reportable to NTSB. Threshold: NTSB 49 CFR Part 830.

For: terminology.

## When to report

Immediately (NTSB phone):
- Fatality / serious injury.
- Substantial damage.
- Engine failure (commercial).
- Aircraft missing.

Within 10 days written: less severe events.

For: legal duty.

## Investigation process

1. On-scene examination.
2. Flight data recorder (FDR) + cockpit voice recorder (CVR) recovery.
3. Maintenance + crew records review.
4. Weather/air-traffic-control evaluation.
5. Reconstruction.
6. Public hearings (major accidents).
7. Final report with probable cause.

Timeline: 12-24 months typical.

For: depth understanding.

## Famous investigations

- **TWA 800 (1996).** Fuel tank explosion; led to inerting requirements.
- **9/11 (2001).** Led to cockpit door + security changes.
- **AF447 (2009).** Loss of attitude indication; pilot procedures.
- **Asiana 214 (2013).** Approach automation reliance.
- **737 MAX MCAS (2018-19).** Software + training failures.

Each reshaped industry.

For: history lessons.

## Recommendations

NTSB issues recommendations to:
- FAA (most common).
- Operators.
- Manufacturers.
- Pilot organizations.

FAA must respond within 90 days (accept, partially accept, decline).

For: change mechanism.

## Most-wanted list

NTSB annual list of safety improvements:
- Common 2024 items: weather safety, mental health awareness, ATC technology.

Industry attention focuser.

For: priorities.

## Pilot deviations

Less serious than accidents:
- Airspace incursion.
- Altitude deviation.
- Wrong runway.

May result in:
- Counseling.
- Remedial training.
- Certificate suspension.
- Fines.

For: less-severe consequences.

## ASRS

Aviation Safety Reporting System (NASA-administered):
- Confidential pilot self-reports.
- Limited immunity from FAA action.
- Used to identify trends.

Encourages openness.

For: learning culture.

## Just culture

Aviation safety culture emphasizes:
- Reporting errors.
- Learning, not punishment.
- Systems thinking.

Vs. blame culture which suppresses reporting.

For: cultural model.

## FAA enforcement

Range:
- Warning.
- Counseling.
- Remedial training.
- Certificate suspension.
- Certificate revocation.
- Civil penalties (fines).

Pilots have appeal process.

For: legal weight.

## TSA + security

After 9/11:
- Reinforced cockpit doors.
- Federal Flight Deck Officer program.
- TSA passenger screening.
- Air marshals.

Affects all commercial aviation.

For: security context.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Not reporting accidents.** Federal violation.
- **No flight data.** Can't reconstruct.
- **Ignoring NTSB recommendations.** Future accidents repeat.

## Summary

- NTSB investigates accidents; independent of FAA.
- Probable cause + recommendations issued.
- Famous investigations reshaped industry.
- ASRS for confidential reporting.
- Just culture vs. blame culture.
