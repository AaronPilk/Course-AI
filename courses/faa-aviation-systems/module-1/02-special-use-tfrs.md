# Special use airspace + TFRs

## Prohibited areas

Marked "P-" on charts. No flight permitted at any time.

Examples: P-56A (over Capitol/White House), P-49 (Bush ranch when president was there).

For: legal off-limits.

## Restricted areas

Marked "R-" on charts. Flight restricted; may be allowed when not active.

Often: military training, weapons ranges.

Check NOTAMs or contact controlling agency.

For: timing-dependent.

## MOAs (Military Operations Areas)

Military training. VFR allowed but caution advised. Check status with FSS or ATC.

Active MOA = fast military jets practicing.

For: shared use.

## Warning areas

Like MOAs but extending offshore (>3 NM from coast). International airspace.

For: offshore military.

## Alert areas

High concentration of pilot training. Both military + civilian.

For: heads-up zones.

## Controlled Firing Areas (CFAs)

Activities suspended if non-participating aircraft approach. Not charted.

For: invisible safety zones.

## TFRs (Temporary Flight Restrictions)

Issued via NOTAM for:
- Disasters (forest fires, floods).
- VIP movement (Presidential).
- Sporting events.
- Space launches.
- Major news events.

Often 3-5 NM radius, surface to several thousand feet.

Check FAA TFR page or ForeFlight before every flight.

For: temporary danger zones.

## VIP TFRs

When President travels:
- 30 NM radius "outer ring" (no VFR without clearance).
- 10 NM "inner ring" (no flight without clearance + transponder).
- 1 NM "no fly" at center.

Massive disruption to GA aviation in area.

For: presidential security.

## Stadium TFRs

NFL/NCAA/NASCAR events:
- 3 NM radius.
- Surface to 3,000 ft AGL.
- 1 hour before, during, 1 hour after.

Active for big games + races.

For: crowd safety.

## Fire TFRs

Around active forest fires:
- Protect firefighting aircraft.
- Drones often violate; significant fines.

Critical to check before flying near smoke.

For: emergency ops.

## NOTAMs

Notice to Air Missions (formerly "Notices to Airmen"):
- Airport closures.
- Runway repairs.
- ILS outages.
- Special events.
- TFRs.

Read all NOTAMs for your route. Required by FAR 91.103.

For: situational awareness.

## ADIZ (Air Defense Identification Zone)

Around coasts + Alaska:
- Filed flight plan required.
- Transponder + comms.
- Penalties for non-compliance.

National security.

For: border airspace.

## SFRA (Special Flight Rules Area)

Most famous: Washington DC SFRA.
- Special training + identifier required.
- All aircraft monitored.
- 60 NM "FRZ" (Flight Restriction Zone) tighter.

Post-9/11 restrictions.

For: high-security urban areas.

## Penalties

Airspace violations:
- $1,000-100,000+ fines.
- Pilot certificate suspension.
- Criminal charges (extreme cases).
- Drones shot down or netted.

Don't violate. Plan thoroughly.

For: legal weight.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Skipping NOTAM check.** Hit a TFR.
- **Assuming MOA inactive.** Get scared by F-16.
- **Ignoring sectional chart edges.** Bust class boundaries.

## Summary

- Prohibited (always closed), Restricted (sometimes), MOA (military training).
- TFRs: temporary; check NOTAMs every flight.
- Presidential TFRs huge; sports/fire TFRs common.
- DC SFRA + ADIZ for security.
- NOTAMs mandatory pre-flight reading.
