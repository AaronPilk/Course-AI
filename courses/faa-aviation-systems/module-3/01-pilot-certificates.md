# Pilot certificates: Private to ATP

## Certificate ladder

- **Student.** Learning; solo authorized.
- **Sport.** Light Sport Aircraft (LSA); 20 hrs min.
- **Recreational.** Limited range; rare.
- **Private (PPL).** Standard entry; 40 hrs min, 60-80 hrs typical.
- **Commercial.** Get paid; 250 hrs.
- **ATP.** Airline Transport Pilot; 1,500 hrs.

For: progression.

## Private Pilot

Most common starting cert. Allows:
- Carry passengers.
- Fly anywhere weather permits.
- No compensation for piloting (cost-sharing OK).

Requirements:
- 17 years old.
- Class 3 medical (or BasicMed).
- 40 hrs flight (incl. 20 dual, 10 solo).
- Written + practical exam.

Cost: $10-20k typical.

For: GA gateway.

## Commercial Pilot

Allows getting paid:
- Banner towing.
- Pipeline patrol.
- Some charter (with restrictions).

Requirements:
- 18 years.
- 250 hrs total time.
- Class 2 medical.
- More demanding flight maneuvers.

For: career start.

## ATP

Required to fly Part 121 (scheduled airlines):
- 1,500 hrs total (1,000 for some 4-year aviation degrees).
- 23 years old.
- Class 1 medical.
- ATP-CTP course before written.

Largest single barrier to airline career.

For: airline gate.

## CFI (Certified Flight Instructor)

Teach others:
- CFI: basic instructor.
- CFII: instrument instructor.
- MEI: multi-engine instructor.

How most pilots build hours toward ATP. ~$30-60/hour pay typical.

For: career path.

## Ratings vs. certificates

- **Certificate.** Base level (private, commercial).
- **Rating.** Additional privilege (instrument, multi-engine, type rating for specific aircraft).

Example: Private Pilot with Instrument + Multi-Engine ratings.

For: terminology.

## Instrument rating

Add to private (or above):
- 50 hrs PIC cross-country.
- 40 hrs instrument (15 with CFII).
- Pass instrument written + practical.

Required for IFR flying.

For: weather flexibility.

## Multi-engine rating

Fly aircraft with 2+ engines:
- ~5-10 hrs training.
- Practical test.
- Often added during commercial.

For: complex aircraft.

## Type ratings

Required for:
- Aircraft >12,500 lbs.
- Turbojet aircraft.
- Specific make/model (e.g., 737, A320).

Costs $5-30k typically; airline often pays.

For: jet flying.

## Recency

To carry passengers:
- 3 takeoffs + landings in last 90 days.
- Night: 3 T/L in last 90 days (full stop at night).

To fly IFR:
- 6 instrument approaches in last 6 months.
- Plus holds + intercepting/tracking courses.

For: stay current.

## Flight reviews

Every 24 months:
- 1 hour ground.
- 1 hour flight.
- With CFI.
- No "pass/fail."

For: ongoing competency.

## International pilots

ICAO licenses similar:
- PPL → EASA PPL etc.
- Conversions possible but often partial retest.

For: cross-border.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Stopping at one cert.** Limits hire-ability.
- **Skipping currency.** Illegal to fly.
- **Old medical.** Class certificate has duration limits.

## Summary

- Student → Sport → Private → Commercial → ATP.
- Add ratings (instrument, multi, type) for capabilities.
- CFI to teach + build hours.
- Currency + flight reviews ongoing.
- Cost: $10-100k+ to ATP.
