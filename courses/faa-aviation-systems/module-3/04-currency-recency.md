# Currency + recency

## Why required

Skills degrade without practice. FAA requires periodic refresh to maintain privileges.

For: safety floor.

## Pilot in command (PIC) currency

To act as PIC carrying passengers:
- 3 takeoffs + landings in last 90 days.
- In same category, class, type.

If lapsed: solo flight only until current.

For: passenger restriction.

## Night currency

To carry passengers at night:
- 3 takeoffs + landings to full stop at night in last 90 days.
- Night = 1 hour after sunset to 1 hour before sunrise.

Without: only solo at night.

For: night-specific.

## Tailwheel currency

Often forgotten:
- 3 T/L in tailwheel within 90 days.
- Otherwise solo only.

For: tailwheel pilots.

## Instrument currency

To act as PIC under IFR (or in IMC):
- 6 instrument approaches in last 6 months.
- Holding procedures.
- Intercepting + tracking courses.

Practiced or actual count. Sim allowed.

For: IFR PIC.

## Instrument Proficiency Check (IPC)

If lapsed IFR currency >12 months:
- Cannot regain by self-practice.
- Need IPC with CFII or examiner.
- Comprehensive instrument review.

For: lapsed instrument.

## Flight review

Every 24 calendar months:
- 1 hour ground.
- 1 hour flight.
- With authorized CFI.
- No pass/fail; signed off when complete.

WINGS program credit option.

For: ongoing competency.

## ATP currency

For Part 121 airline:
- Currency requirements stricter.
- Type-specific sim every 6-9 months.
- Line checks.
- Recurrent training.

For: airline ops.

## High-altitude / pressurized currency

If endorsement-required aircraft:
- Stays current with regular flight.
- No special requirement after endorsement.

For: jet-rated pilots.

## Medical timing

Must have:
- Valid medical for date of flight.
- Class matching highest level used.

Expired medical = no PIC privileges.

For: medical-rating link.

## Lapsed certificate

If certificates expire without renewal:
- Practical test required to regain.
- Knowledge test if >2 years lapsed.

For: don't let lapse.

## Recovery options

Lost currency? Options:
- Fly solo (PPL+) to regain.
- Flight review (CFI).
- IPC for instrument.
- Type ride for type rating.

For: getting back legal.

## Logbook

Required to track:
- Total time.
- PIC vs. SIC.
- Cross-country.
- Instrument time (actual + simulated).
- Day vs. night.
- Aircraft category/class.

Electronic (ForeFlight) or paper.

For: proof.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Letting currency lapse before trip.** Stuck.
- **Bad logbook math.** Currency calc errors.
- **Skipping medical.** Illegal.

## Summary

- 3 T/L in 90 days for PIC w/ passengers.
- 3 T/L night for night passengers.
- 6 approaches + holds for IFR.
- Flight review every 24 months.
- IPC for lapsed instrument >12 months.
- Logbook + medical always current.
