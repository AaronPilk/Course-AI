# Medical certificates + BasicMed

## Medical classes

- **Class 1.** Required for ATP. Strictest; 1-year validity (under 40), 6 months (40+).
- **Class 2.** Commercial Pilots. 1 year.
- **Class 3.** Private Pilots. 5 years under 40, 2 years over 40.

For: matching cert to medical.

## AME (Aviation Medical Examiner)

FAA-designated doctor performs exam:
- Standard medical workup.
- Vision tested.
- BP, hearing.
- ECG (Class 1 over 40).
- Submits results to FAA via MedXPress.

For: process.

## Common disqualifications

- **Cardiovascular.** Heart disease, surgery.
- **Mental health.** Some medications/diagnoses.
- **Substance abuse.**
- **Eyesight.** Some vision issues.
- **Diabetes.** Especially insulin-dependent.
- **Epilepsy.**

Special Issuance (SI) sometimes granted with documentation.

For: be honest in exam.

## BasicMed

Alternative to Class 3 for some pilots:
- Hold valid FAA medical ever.
- See physician every 4 years.
- Take FAA online course every 2 years.
- Limited to 6 passengers, under 18,000 ft, under 250 kts, US/Mexico/Bahamas.

Available since 2017.

For: many private pilots.

## Medical Special Issuance

FAA can grant medical even with disqualifying condition if:
- Stable.
- Documentation extensive.
- Periodic monitoring.

Common for: depression (some SSRIs OK), heart issues, etc.

For: when standard medical denied.

## Sport pilot medical

Only requires driver's license:
- No FAA medical exam.
- Limited to Light Sport Aircraft.
- Day VFR.

For: lowest barrier.

## Pre-flight medical self-check

IMSAFE checklist:
- **I**llness.
- **M**edication.
- **S**tress.
- **A**lcohol.
- **F**atigue.
- **E**motion.

Every flight. Self-grounding if any issue.

For: daily check.

## Alcohol + drugs

FAA rules:
- 8-hour bottle-to-throttle (FAR 91.17).
- BAC must be 0.04% or less.
- No flying under influence.
- DOT testing for commercial pilots.

For: regulations.

## Mental health stigma

Historically pilots hid issues fearing certificate loss. Modern shift:
- More mental health-friendly process.
- FAA HIMS program for substance abuse recovery.
- Encouraged to seek help.

For: cultural change.

## When to lose medical

Required to self-report:
- New diagnosis.
- New medication.
- Hospitalization.
- Substance abuse.

Failure to report is grounds for revocation.

For: legal duty.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Lying on medical.** Worse than disqualification.
- **Self-treating to pass.** Dangerous.
- **Ignoring expiration.** Illegal to fly.

## Summary

- Class 1 (ATP), Class 2 (commercial), Class 3 (private).
- Validity varies by age + class.
- BasicMed alternative for many private pilots.
- IMSAFE pre-flight self-check.
- 8-hour bottle-to-throttle.
- Special Issuance for some conditions.
