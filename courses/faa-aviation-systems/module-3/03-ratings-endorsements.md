# Ratings + endorsements

## Ratings overview

Add to certificate; expand privileges:
- **Instrument.** Fly IFR.
- **Multi-Engine (ME).** Aircraft with 2+ engines.
- **Type Rating.** Specific large aircraft (737, A320).
- **Glider.** Powered or unpowered.
- **Rotorcraft (Helicopter, Gyroplane).**
- **Lighter-than-air.** Balloons, airships.
- **Powered-lift.** Tilt-rotor (Osprey-style).

For: capability expansion.

## Instrument rating

Most useful add-on:
- Fly through clouds.
- Filed IFR clearance.
- Maintains career options.

Training: ~50 hrs flight + ~30 hrs ground. $8-15k typical.

For: weather flexibility.

## Multi-Engine rating

Twin-engine aircraft:
- More complex (engine-out emergencies).
- Required for many career paths.
- Cessna 310, Beechcraft Baron typical training.

Cost: $2-5k.

For: career stepping.

## Endorsements

Logbook signatures unlocking specific:
- **High performance.** >200 HP.
- **Complex.** Retractable gear, controllable prop, flaps.
- **Tailwheel.** Conventional gear aircraft.
- **High altitude.** Pressurized aircraft.
- **TAA (Technically Advanced Aircraft).** Glass cockpit.

CFI-signed; no separate exam.

For: aircraft-specific.

## Sea plane rating

For aircraft operating from water:
- Single-engine sea (SES).
- Multi-engine sea (MES).
- Special "splash and dash" practice.

For: adventurous flying.

## Aerobatic / formation

No FAA-required rating but training recommended:
- Aerobatic competence.
- Formation flying.
- Helps in upset recovery.

For: skill expansion.

## Commercial / ATP specific

- Commercial: longer cross-countries, more demanding maneuvers.
- ATP: airline-style operations, more theory, ATP-CTP course.

For: career progression.

## Type rating example

Boeing 737:
- ~3 weeks training + simulator.
- Type-specific FAA practical.
- ~$25-35k if paid personally; airlines cover.

For: airline transition.

## Foreign licenses

Conversions to FAA require:
- ICAO valid.
- Written exam (US theory).
- Practical exam.
- Document verification.

For: international pilots.

## Career timeline example

- 0 hrs: Start training.
- 60 hrs: PPL.
- 100 hrs: Instrument.
- 150 hrs: Commercial.
- 200 hrs: Multi + CFI.
- 200-1,500 hrs: Build hours as CFI.
- 1,500 hrs: ATP minimums.
- Apply to regional airlines.
- Eventually move to majors.

3-5 years typical.

For: timeline expectations.

## 121 vs 135 vs 91

Regulations differ:
- **Part 121.** Scheduled airline.
- **Part 135.** On-demand charter, fractional.
- **Part 91.** Private, GA.

Different rules; certificates may differ.

For: ops category.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Pursuing one rating only.** Limited.
- **Skipping instrument.** Limits weather options.
- **No tailwheel exposure.** Many backcountry aircraft.

## Summary

- Add ratings: Instrument, Multi-Engine, Type.
- Endorsements (CFI-signed) for specific aircraft.
- Sea/glider/heli ratings for niches.
- Type rating for jets ($25-35k).
- Career timeline: 3-5 years from zero to airlines.
