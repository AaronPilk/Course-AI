# What a robot is

## Definition

Robot = autonomous or semi-autonomous machine that senses environment, processes information, and acts upon the world.

Three pillars:
- **Sense.** Perceive via sensors.
- **Think.** Process via controllers.
- **Act.** Respond via actuators.

For: framework.

## Categories

- **Industrial robots.** Manufacturing arms (KUKA, ABB, Fanuc).
- **Mobile robots.** Self-driving (Tesla, Waymo), drones, AGVs.
- **Service robots.** Cleaning (Roomba), delivery (Starship), restaurant.
- **Humanoid robots.** Boston Dynamics Atlas, Figure 01, Tesla Optimus.
- **Medical robots.** Da Vinci surgical, exoskeletons.
- **Research / education.** TurtleBot, Crazyflie drones.

For: scope of field.

## Key disciplines

- **Mechanical engineering.** Body, joints, materials.
- **Electrical engineering.** Power, motors, electronics.
- **Computer science.** Software, perception, planning.
- **Control theory.** Mathematical control of dynamics.
- **AI/ML.** Learned behavior, neural networks.

Robotics is interdisciplinary; specialists work together.

For: skill landscape.

## Levels of autonomy

- **Tele-operated.** Human directly controls (excavator).
- **Assisted.** Human + AI together (Tesla Autopilot).
- **Semi-autonomous.** Robot does most; human supervises.
- **Fully autonomous.** Robot decides + acts alone.

Higher autonomy = harder design.

For: maturity gauge.

## Robotics vs. AI

AI ≠ robotics. AI is the brain; robotics is the body + brain. Many AI systems have no body (LLMs, chess engines). Robots use AI but also need hardware integration.

For: distinction.

## Modern roboticist skills

Daily work:
- ROS 2 + Python/C++.
- Linux (Ubuntu typical).
- Git + CI.
- Math: linear algebra, calculus, probability.
- ML basics for perception.

## Hard problems remaining

- **Manipulation.** Picking arbitrary objects.
- **Locomotion.** Walking robustly on varied terrain.
- **Generalization.** Operating in unseen environments.
- **Safety.** Around humans + critical systems.

Active research areas.

## Career paths

- **R&D engineer.** Research labs (Toyota Research, NVIDIA).
- **Robotics software.** ROS / motion planning.
- **Computer vision.** Perception specialists.
- **ML/RL.** Learned control.
- **Hardware engineer.** Mechatronics.

Median US salary: $130-220k+ for senior roles.

For: career planning.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Treating it as software-only.** Hardware matters.
- **Ignoring math.** Linear algebra + dynamics essential.
- **Skipping simulation.** Iterate in sim before hardware.

## Summary

- Robot = sense + think + act.
- Multi-disciplinary: mech + EE + CS + control + ML.
- Major categories: industrial, mobile, service, humanoid, medical.
- Hard problems: manipulation, locomotion, generalization, safety.
