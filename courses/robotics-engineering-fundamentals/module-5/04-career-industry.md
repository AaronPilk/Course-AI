# Career + industry trends

## Career paths

- **Robotics software engineer.** ROS, motion planning, perception. $130-220k.
- **Computer vision engineer.** Perception specialization. $140-230k.
- **ML/RL engineer for robotics.** Learning policies. $180-300k.
- **Hardware engineer (mechatronics).** Mechanical + EE. $120-200k.
- **Controls engineer.** PID, MPC, dynamics. $130-220k.
- **R&D / research scientist.** Often PhD. $150-300k+.
- **Product manager (robotics).** PM + technical depth. $150-280k.

US salary ranges 2026.

For: career planning.

## Education paths

- **CS / EE degree.** Solid foundation.
- **Mechanical engineering.** Pair with software.
- **MS in Robotics.** CMU, MIT, Georgia Tech, ETH Zurich.
- **Online.** Stanford CS 223A, Coursera Robotics specialization.
- **PhD for research positions.**

Many roles don't require PhD.

For: education strategy.

## Job market

Growing sectors:
- **Humanoids.** Tesla, Figure, Boston Dynamics, etc.
- **Warehouse automation.** Amazon, Symbotic.
- **Surgical robotics.** Intuitive, CMR, Stryker.
- **Defense.** Anduril, Boston Dynamics defense.
- **Self-driving.** Waymo, Cruise.

Stagnant:
- Pure industrial arm.

For: opportunity mapping.

## Portfolio building

For job applications:
- ROS package on GitHub.
- Demo videos of robots you built.
- Conference papers (RSS, ICRA, IROS, RA-L).
- Open-source contributions.

Stronger than just resume.

For: standing out.

## Top companies

- **Boston Dynamics.** Iconic.
- **Tesla Optimus team.** Humanoid.
- **Figure.** Humanoid.
- **NVIDIA Robotics.** Isaac, simulation.
- **Toyota Research.** R&D.
- **Skydio.** Drones.
- **Anduril.** Defense.
- **Apptronik.** Apollo humanoid.

For: target companies.

## Conferences + papers

Major venues:
- **ICRA.** International Conference on Robotics + Automation.
- **IROS.** International Conference on Intelligent Robots + Systems.
- **RSS.** Robotics Science + Systems.
- **CoRL.** Conference on Robot Learning.
- **RA-L.** Robotics + Automation Letters (journal).

For: staying current.

## Open source contribution

Major projects:
- ROS 2 itself (https://github.com/ros2).
- MoveIt 2.
- Navigation 2.
- Cartographer.

Contributing builds skills + network + portfolio.

For: career boost.

## Specialization vs. generalist

- **Generalist.** Smaller startups; multiple hats.
- **Specialist.** Large companies; deep expertise.

Both viable. Specialty in high-demand area (RL, foundation models, manipulation) commands premium.

For: positioning.

## Foundation models impact

VLA models (RT-2, OpenVLA, Octo) changing field:
- Less task-specific programming.
- More data + scale dependence.
- New careers around data curation + RLHF for robots.

Stay learning; field changes fast.

For: future-proofing.

## Industry trends 2026

- **Humanoid mass production.** Tesla / Figure / Apptronik shipping.
- **Foundation models for control.** Generalist robots emerging.
- **End-to-end learned policies.** Replacing module pipelines.
- **Robotics-as-a-Service.** Lease vs. buy.
- **AI safety standards.** Regulation forming.

For: where field heading.

## Demographics

Robotics field still:
- Male-dominated (~85%).
- US + China + Japan + Germany.
- Aging in industrial; younger in startups.

Diversity push ongoing.

For: cultural awareness.

## Continuous learning

Resources:
- **The Construct.** ROS tutorials.
- **Robotics StackExchange.** Q&A.
- **r/robotics.** Reddit community.
- **YouTube: Robotics Channel, Two Minute Papers.**
- **Books.** Modern Robotics (Lynch), Probabilistic Robotics (Thrun).

For: skill development.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Specializing too early.** Get broad foundation first.
- **Only theory.** Build real robots.
- **Ignoring soft skills.** Robotics = team sport.

## Summary

- Many career paths; software + ML + hardware all needed.
- Build portfolio: GitHub + videos + papers.
- Humanoids + foundation models are growth areas.
- ICRA / IROS / RSS for staying current.
- Open source contribution boosts everything.

That's the course. Next: build real robots, contribute to ROS 2, join conferences.
