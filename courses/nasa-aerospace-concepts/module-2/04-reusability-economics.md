# Reusability + cost economics

## Pre-reusability era

Saturn V (1969): $185M per launch (~$1.4B today). Built + thrown away.

Space Shuttle (1981-2011): refurbished but $1.5B per launch.

Result: spaceflight rare + expensive.

For: context.

## SpaceX reusability

Falcon 9 first reuse: 2017. Now routine:
- ~12+ reflights per booster (B1062 record).
- Refurbishment days/weeks not months.
- 30-50% cost reduction vs. expendable.

For: paradigm shift.

## Reuse types

- **Booster.** First stage returns. SpaceX, Blue Origin.
- **Fairings.** Recovered + reused. SpaceX.
- **Capsule.** Crew Dragon, Cargo Dragon refly.
- **Full vehicle.** Starship target.

For: incremental reuse.

## Landing methods

Falcon 9 first stage:
- Boostback burn → return to launch site (or ASDS at sea).
- Entry burn → slow before atmospheric heat.
- Landing burn → 1-3 engines to land vertically.

For: return architecture.

## Economic model

Cost per launch:
- Marginal cost (fuel + ops): ~$1-2M.
- Booster amortization (~10 flights): $5-10M.
- Stage 2 (expendable): $10M.
- Total: $20-30M for paying customer at $67M.

Higher reuse → lower cost.

For: economics.

## Starship target

SpaceX goal:
- Full reuse (booster + ship).
- 100+ flights per article.
- ~$10/kg to LEO (vs. Falcon 9 $2,700/kg).

If achieved: 200× cost reduction.

For: future ambition.

## Tyranny of distance vs. cost

Launching to LEO:
- Saturn V: $50,000/kg.
- Falcon 9 reused: $2,700/kg.
- Starship target: $10/kg.

Beyond LEO multiplies by 5-20×. Mars-bound payload still expensive.

For: real-world cost.

## Commercial vs. government

Government-built rockets:
- Cost-plus contracts inflate prices.
- Decades of development.
- Few launches.

Commercial:
- Fixed-price contracts.
- Years of development.
- Many launches.

SpaceX, Rocket Lab, Blue Origin, Stoke Space, Relativity competing.

For: market evolution.

## SLS vs. Starship

NASA's Space Launch System:
- 70-95 tons to LEO.
- $4.1B per launch.
- Expendable.

SpaceX Starship:
- 100-150 tons to LEO (target).
- ~$100M per launch (target).
- Fully reusable.

40× cost difference if Starship works.

For: contemporary comparison.

## Falcon Heavy + payload niches

Falcon 9: ~22 tons LEO, $67M.
Falcon Heavy: ~64 tons LEO, $97M.
Starship: 100-150 tons LEO, ~$100M target.

Each occupies different niche.

For: vehicle selection.

## Mass-to-orbit revolution

Pre-SpaceX: ~250 tons launched globally per year.
2023: ~1,500 tons (60% SpaceX).
2025+: 5,000+ tons projected with Starship.

Total mass launched changing entire industry.

For: scale shift.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Comparing cost per launch instead of $/kg.** Reuse dilutes per-flight.
- **Believing without flying.** Demo important.
- **Ignoring refurbishment cost.** Not just fuel.

## Summary

- Reusability halves cost; Starship targets 1000× reduction.
- Falcon 9 fully operational; Starship in test.
- Government cost-plus vs. commercial fixed-price.
- Mass launched per year quintupling.
- Spaceflight transitioning from rare to routine.
