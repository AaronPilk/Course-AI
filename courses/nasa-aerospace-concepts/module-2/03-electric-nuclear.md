# Electric + nuclear propulsion

## Electric propulsion principle

Use electricity to accelerate ionized propellant. Very high exhaust velocity → high Isp (~3,000+ s).

But: very low thrust (~50 mN typical). Acts over months/years.

For: deep-space slow-build delta-v.

## Ion thrusters

Xenon ionized; accelerated through grid:
- Thrust: 50-300 mN.
- Isp: 2,500-4,000 s.

Used by: Dawn (Vesta + Ceres), DART, BepiColombo.

For: science missions with patience.

## Hall thrusters

Plasma accelerated by E×B field:
- Higher thrust than ion (~100 mN-2 N).
- Lower Isp (1,500-3,000 s).

Used by: Starlink for stationkeeping + deorbit.

For: practical satellite use.

## Solar electric

Solar panels power electric thruster. Dependent on solar distance.

Inner solar system (Mercury to Mars): viable.
Outer system: requires huge arrays or alternative.

For: typical electric power source.

## Nuclear thermal propulsion

Reactor heats hydrogen → expanded through nozzle.

- Isp: ~900 s (2× chemical).
- High thrust (like chemical).
- Reactor + radiation shielding heavy.

NERVA tested in 1960s. NASA + DARPA reviving for Mars (DRACO program).

For: high-thrust + good Isp combo.

## Nuclear electric propulsion

Reactor → electricity → ion thruster.

- Isp: 5,000-10,000 s.
- Low thrust like solar electric.
- No solar power dependence.

Concepts; not flown.

For: outer solar system future.

## Solar sails

Photons reflect off large mirror → tiny push:
- No propellant!
- Lightweight.
- Slow acceleration.

IKAROS (2010), LightSail-2 (2019). Breakthrough Starshot proposed for interstellar.

For: propellantless option.

## Comparison

| | Thrust | Isp | Best for |
|--|--------|-----|---------|
| Chemical | High | Low | Launch + maneuvers |
| Hall electric | Low | Medium | Stationkeeping |
| Ion | Very low | High | Deep space |
| Nuclear thermal | High | Medium-high | Mars |
| Solar sail | Tiny | Infinite | Patient missions |

For: choice by mission.

## DART mission

NASA's planetary defense test (2022):
- Asteroid impact for orbit deflection.
- Used Hall thruster (NEXT-C) for fine maneuvers.

Successful: changed Didymos' orbit measurably.

For: real electric propulsion use.

## BepiColombo to Mercury

ESA + JAXA mission to Mercury (2018 launch). Uses 4 ion thrusters + 9 gravity assists (Earth, Venus, Mercury).

Arrives 2026. Total delta-v from launch: ~5+ km/s of ion power.

For: complex multi-thruster mission.

## Future: fusion propulsion

Hypothetical fusion engines:
- Isp: 100,000+ s.
- Real interstellar capable.
- Decades away.

For: long-term aspiration.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Using ion for launch.** Can't lift off Earth.
- **Forgetting power supply weight.** Ion needs MWs.
- **Ignoring solar distance.** Solar electric fails at outer planets.

## Summary

- Electric (ion, Hall) high Isp, low thrust → deep space.
- Nuclear thermal in development; Mars potential.
- Solar sails propellantless.
- Match propulsion to mission profile.
- BepiColombo, DART, Starlink use electric daily.
