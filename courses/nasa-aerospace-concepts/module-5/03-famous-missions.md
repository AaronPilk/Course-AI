# Famous missions

## Apollo (1969-1972)

- 6 successful crewed Moon landings.
- 12 people on the Moon.
- 380 kg lunar samples returned.
- Saturn V: most powerful rocket flown to date.
- Engineering achievement of the 20th century.

For: human spaceflight pinnacle.

## Voyager 1 + 2 (1977-)

- Launched 1977; still operating.
- Visited Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune.
- Voyager 1 entered interstellar space (2012).
- Powered by RTGs (3 each).
- Carrying "Golden Record" with Earth sounds.

For: longest-running mission.

## Mars rovers

- **Sojourner (1997).** First; ~12 kg.
- **Spirit / Opportunity (2004).** Designed 90 days; lasted 6 + 15 years.
- **Curiosity (2012).** Sky Crane debut; still operating.
- **Perseverance (2021).** Collecting samples for future return; carries Ingenuity (first off-Earth helicopter).

For: Mars exploration arc.

## Cassini-Huygens (1997-2017)

- Orbited Saturn 13 years.
- Discovered Enceladus's ocean.
- Discovered Titan's methane lakes.
- Probed dropped on Titan (first outer-solar-system landing).
- Ended with "grand finale" dive into Saturn's atmosphere.

For: outer solar system exploration.

## ISS (1998-)

- $150B+ international project.
- Continuously crewed since 2000.
- 6 partners: NASA, Russia, ESA, Japan, Canada, Europe.
- 200+ astronauts visited.
- Major contribution: medical, materials research, technology demonstrations.

For: largest space construction.

## Hubble Space Telescope (1990-)

- 4 servicing missions extended life.
- 1.5+ million observations.
- Photographed deepest fields ever.
- Mirror flaw (spherical aberration) → corrective optics installed by astronauts.

For: science + repair.

## JWST (2021-)

- 100× more powerful than Hubble.
- At L2; sees infrared.
- Cost: $10B.
- Mid-IR instruments at 7 K (cryocooler).
- First images late 2022; revolutionizing astronomy.

For: current state-of-art.

## SpaceX milestones

- 2010: First commercial spacecraft to LEO.
- 2012: First commercial cargo to ISS.
- 2015: First successful rocket recovery.
- 2020: First commercial crew to ISS.
- 2023+: Starship test flights.

For: commercial era.

## Recent

- **Artemis I (2022).** Uncrewed lunar flyby.
- **DART (2022).** Successful asteroid deflection.
- **Psyche (2023).** Metallic asteroid mission.
- **Europa Clipper (2024).** En route.
- **Starship integrated flight tests (2023+).** Reusability proof.

For: current ops.

## Failed / lessons-learned

- **Challenger (1986).** O-ring + cold.
- **Columbia (2003).** Foam strike.
- **Mars Climate Orbiter (1999).** Units mismatch.
- **Mars Polar Lander (1999).** Sensor noise triggered shutdown.
- **Beagle 2 (2003).** Mars; lost on landing.

Each taught vital lessons.

For: humility + learning.

## Future ambitions

- Artemis returns humans to Moon (2026-2028).
- Mars Sample Return.
- Habitable Worlds Observatory (Hubble + JWST successor).
- Crewed Mars (2030s-2040s).
- Permanent lunar base.

For: looking forward.

## Lessons from missions

- Conservative design wins.
- Test, test, test.
- Redundancy + cross-checks.
- Communicate problems early.
- Public communication shapes funding.

For: engineering wisdom.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Cutting corners under schedule pressure.** Challenger.
- **Single-string critical systems.** Mars Climate Orbiter.
- **Treating "lessons" as one-time.** Same mistakes repeat.

## Summary

- Apollo: pinnacle of crewed exploration.
- Voyager: longest-running, interstellar.
- Mars rovers: continuous robotic presence.
- ISS: international cooperation.
- Hubble + JWST: revolutionary science.
- Failures teach as much as successes.
