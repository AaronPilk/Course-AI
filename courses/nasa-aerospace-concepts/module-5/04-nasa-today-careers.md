# NASA today + careers

## NASA structure

10 field centers across US:
- **JPL.** Robotic missions; Mars, deep space.
- **JSC (Houston).** Human spaceflight.
- **KSC (Florida).** Launch operations.
- **GSFC (Goddard).** Earth + astrophysics.
- **MSFC (Marshall).** Propulsion + SLS.
- **GRC (Glenn).** Aeronautics + propulsion.
- **LaRC (Langley).** Aeronautics + atmospheric.
- **ARC (Ames).** Software + astrobiology.
- **AFRC (Armstrong).** Flight research.
- **Stennis.** Engine testing.

For: institutional landscape.

## Programs

- **Artemis.** Return to Moon by 2028.
- **Mars Sample Return.** Collect + return samples.
- **Earth Science.** Climate satellites.
- **Astrophysics.** JWST + future telescopes.
- **Heliophysics.** Solar Probe Plus etc.
- **Aeronautics.** Quiet supersonic X-59.
- **Commercial Crew + Cargo.** Public-private.

For: where work happens.

## Commercial space rise

2010s shift:
- SpaceX dominant launch (60%+ market).
- Blue Origin, Rocket Lab, ULA, Northrop, Arianespace competing.
- New entrants: Stoke Space, Relativity, Firefly.
- Constellations: Starlink, OneWeb, Kuiper.

For: vibrant market.

## Career paths

- **NASA civil servant.** Stable; PhD often required.
- **NASA contractor (Lockheed, Boeing, JPL Caltech).** Many roles.
- **SpaceX / Blue Origin.** Fast-paced.
- **New space startup.** High risk, high reward.
- **Government / DoD.** Space Force, intelligence.
- **Academia.** Research + teaching.

For: options.

## Salary ranges

US 2026:
- Entry level engineer: $80-110k.
- Mid-career: $120-180k.
- Senior: $180-280k.
- Principal / executive: $250k+.

NASA: lower than commercial; pensions + stability compensate.

For: realistic expectations.

## Education paths

- **BS aerospace / mechanical / EE / CS.** Standard entry.
- **MS often required for engineering roles.**
- **PhD for research positions.**
- **Internships at NASA / contractor critical.**
- **Top schools: MIT, Stanford, Caltech, Georgia Tech, Purdue, Michigan.**

For: prep planning.

## Skills in demand

- Mechanical design + CAD (SolidWorks, NX, CATIA).
- Software (C++, Python, Rust).
- Embedded systems.
- ML for autonomy.
- Systems engineering.
- Materials science (composites, additive).

For: skill investment.

## Getting in

- Apply via USAJobs (NASA civil service).
- Direct to contractors (Lockheed, Boeing).
- Direct to startups.
- Network at conferences: AIAA, IAC.
- Build portfolio: capstone projects, internships.

For: process.

## US space leadership

US still leads in launches (60% globally), but China rising fast:
- 2024: ~60+ Chinese launches.
- 2024: ~140+ US launches (SpaceX dominant).
- Russia: declining.
- Europe: Ariane 6 launched.
- India, Japan, UAE, Korea active.

For: global picture.

## Geopolitics

Space is contested:
- DoD increasingly active.
- Space Force established 2019.
- Concerns about ASAT weapons (anti-satellite).
- Treaty of 1967 limits weapons in space.

For: political reality.

## Privatization vs. NASA-only

NASA shifting:
- Buy launches (don't build).
- Buy services (Commercial Crew).
- Focus on big leaps (Mars, deep space).

Commercial does routine; NASA does exploration.

For: division of labor.

## Public engagement

NASA huge in public consciousness:
- Webb images viral.
- Mars rovers beloved.
- Artemis launch viewed by millions.

Communication matters for funding.

For: outreach importance.

## Future workforce

Hot growth areas 2026+:
- Reusable launch.
- Lunar surface ops.
- Mars architecture.
- Satellite constellations.
- Space resources (mining).
- Space industrialization.

Robotics + autonomy growing fast.

For: where to invest.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Aiming only at NASA civil service.** Commercial pays more, moves faster.
- **Not interning.** Critical for first job.
- **Underestimating soft skills.** Aerospace requires teamwork.

## Summary

- NASA 10 centers; programs span Earth → solar system → universe.
- Commercial space booming alongside NASA.
- Standard education: BS aerospace + internship + grad school.
- Salaries $80-280k+.
- Growth areas: reusable launch, lunar, Mars, AI/autonomy.

That's the course. Next steps: pursue internship, contribute to open-source space projects, attend conferences, build portfolio.
