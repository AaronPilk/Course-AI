# Lagrange points + n-body

## n-body problem

3+ bodies have no closed-form solution. Numerical integration required.

For most missions: simplify to 2-body with perturbations.

For: complexity awareness.

## Lagrange points

5 equilibrium points in 2-body system (e.g., Sun-Earth):

- **L1.** Between bodies; saddle point. 1.5M km from Earth toward Sun.
- **L2.** Opposite side of smaller body. 1.5M km from Earth away from Sun. (JWST is here!)
- **L3.** Opposite Earth across Sun.
- **L4 / L5.** Stable triangular points 60° ahead/behind smaller body in orbit.

For: gravity equilibrium.

## L1 + L2 + L3 instability

Saddle equilibria — small perturbation grows. Stationkeeping fuel needed:
- JWST burns ~3-5 m/s/year for stationkeeping at L2.
- Long-term occupation requires propellant.

For: maintenance cost.

## L4 / L5 stability

Stable for mass ratio < 0.0385. For Sun-Jupiter: yes → Trojan asteroids accumulate at L4/L5.

For Earth-Moon: stable; some interest in colonization.

For: natural accumulation.

## Halo orbits

Orbit around L1 or L2 (not just sitting at point):
- Larger amplitude.
- Naturally avoids Earth's shadow.
- JWST uses L2 halo orbit.

For: practical L-point use.

## Lissajous orbits

Quasi-periodic orbits near L-points. More flexible than halo.

For: alternative mission options.

## Why L2 for telescopes

JWST, Herschel, Planck, future telescopes all at L2:
- Earth + Sun + Moon all in same direction.
- Single sunshade blocks all hot sources.
- Cold side stays cold (~50 K) → infrared sensitive.

For: cold-mission rationale.

## ICE-Roe / WIND missions at L1

Sun-observing missions at L1:
- DSCOVR.
- SOHO.
- ACE.

L1 position 1 hour ahead of Earth in solar wind → early warning.

For: solar observation.

## Sun-Earth vs. Earth-Moon

Lagrange points exist in each pair:
- Sun-Earth L2: JWST.
- Earth-Moon L2: future lunar gateway?

For: bilateral systems.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Sitting exactly at L-point.** Unstable; halo orbit instead.
- **No stationkeeping fuel budget.** Mission lost early.
- **Confusing L1 with L2.** Wrong direction.

## Summary

- 5 Lagrange points per 2-body system.
- L1/L2/L3 saddle (need stationkeeping); L4/L5 stable.
- L2 great for cold telescopes (JWST).
- L1 great for solar wind monitoring.
- Halo orbits practical occupation method.
