# Heat shields + materials

## Ablative heat shields

Carbon-based; vaporize on heating:
- AVCOAT (Apollo).
- PICA / PICA-X (Dragon, Stardust).
- Carbon-phenolic.

Sacrificial: shield loses mass, heat carried away with vapor.

For: deep-space + lunar/Mars return.

## Reusable shields

- **HRSI tiles (Shuttle).** Black ceramic tiles; brittle.
- **TUFROC.** Newer; more robust.
- **PICA-X.** SpaceX modification of PICA; partial reuse.
- **Stainless steel (Starship).** Active cooling experimental.

For: reuse missions.

## Materials properties

Heat shield needs:
- High temperature tolerance.
- Low thermal conductivity (insulate).
- Stable ablation rate (predictable).
- Lightweight.
- Manufacturable.

PICA: 0.27 g/cm³; can handle 8,000°C surface.

For: material trade-offs.

## Shuttle TPS failure

Columbia (2003): leading edge RCC (reinforced carbon-carbon) damaged at launch by foam. During reentry, hot plasma entered wing, destroyed structure.

7 astronauts killed.

For: importance of inspection.

## Mars rover backshell

Curiosity / Perseverance:
- Heat shield: SLA-561V (different from Apollo).
- Designed for Mars atmosphere not Earth.
- Single use.

For: Mars-specific design.

## Capsule shapes

- **Apollo / Orion.** Blunt cone; lifting capsule.
- **Soyuz.** Sphere section.
- **Dragon.** Blunt cone like Apollo.
- **Starliner.** Apollo-like.

All blunt — for the right reason.

For: form factor.

## Heat shield testing

- Wind tunnels (limited to subsonic + low supersonic).
- Plasma torches (long-duration heating).
- Atmospheric reentry demos (sub-scale).
- Sample return missions (Stardust, Hayabusa).

For: pre-flight validation.

## TPS thickness

Apollo capsule heat shield: 6.4 cm thick.

Loses ~3 cm during reentry; remaining is insulation.

For: design margin.

## Future: lightweight shields

Research:
- Inflatable heat shields (HIAD).
- Ultra-high temperature ceramics (UHTC).
- Active cooling.

For: next-gen options.

## Failure modes

- Ablation rate higher than expected → burn through.
- Thermal stress cracking.
- Vibration damage.
- Edge effects (gaps in tiles).

Testing + inspection critical.

For: reliability.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Untested geometry.** Heat distribution unknown.
- **Inadequate margin.** Single-point failure.
- **Skipping inspection.** Columbia lesson.

## Summary

- Ablative shields (PICA-X) for high-energy reentry.
- Reusable tiles (Shuttle) limited material strength.
- Blunt body + thick shield = standard.
- Mars EDL designed for thin atmosphere.
- Inflatable shields emerging.
