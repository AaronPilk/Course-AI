# Landing systems

## Parachute landing

Drogue parachutes slow from supersonic; main chutes for final descent.

Apollo: 3 main chutes ~30 m diameter. Splash velocity 8 m/s.

Dragon: 4 main chutes. Crew + cargo variants splash.

For: passive deceleration.

## Powered landing

Retro engines fire to slow:
- **Falcon 9 first stage.** 1-3 engines vertically. ~6 m/s touchdown.
- **Starship.** Belly-flop then flip + land vertically.
- **Mars Curiosity Sky Crane.** Hover, lower rover on cable.

For: precise landing.

## Water vs. ground landing

Water (US tradition):
- Pros: gentler, easier recovery.
- Cons: capsule corrodes, ship needed.

Ground (Russian tradition):
- Pros: faster recovery, no salt water.
- Cons: harder impact, parachute + retrorockets.

For: tradition choice.

## Soyuz landing

Soyuz lands in Kazakhstan steppe:
- Parachutes slow to ~7 m/s.
- Retrorockets fire ~1 m before touchdown → 1-2 m/s impact.
- Russian style: harsh but reliable.

50+ year track record.

For: proven method.

## Starliner / Crew Dragon

Capsule landing:
- 4 main parachutes deploy.
- Splashdown 8 m/s (water).
- Recovery ship picks up.

Crew Dragon: also recovered from water for reuse.

For: modern US approach.

## Mars rover landings

Curiosity (2012) + Perseverance (2021):
1. Heat shield + supersonic chute.
2. Backshell ejected.
3. Sky Crane: powered rocket platform.
4. Lowers rover on cables (Sky Crane hovers).
5. Cables cut; Sky Crane flies away.

7 minutes of terror.

For: complex multi-stage.

## Lunar landing

Apollo:
- Lunar Module descent engine throttled.
- Pilot manual control final phase.

Modern:
- Artemis Human Landing System (Starship).
- Multiple lunar landers (Intuitive Machines, Astrobotic, etc.).

For: vacuum landing (no parachutes).

## Touchdown impact

Limits:
- Apollo crew: 8 g vertical.
- Crew Dragon: 7 g.
- Soyuz: 10-15 g (Russian tougher).
- Cargo: depends; some 30+ g.

Higher impact = lighter system.

For: design tradeoff.

## Landing accuracy

- Falcon 9 booster: < 10 m at sea (drone ship).
- Crew Dragon: ~3 km radius.
- Soyuz: ~10-30 km radius.
- Mars Curiosity: 20 km × 6 km landing ellipse.
- Perseverance: 7 km × 6 km.
- Improvement curve over 60 years.

For: precision over time.

## Skycrane innovation

Mars rover too heavy for direct rocket landing (engines dig into surface). Sky Crane:
- Powered descent stage.
- Hovers above surface.
- Lowers rover 7m on cables.
- Cuts cables, flies away.

Genius engineering.

For: indirect landing.

## Future: propulsive landing

Trends:
- Starship full propulsive (no parachutes).
- Reusable boosters everywhere.
- Mars human missions need 20+ ton landers.

For: where heading.

## Mistakes to avoid

- **Single string parachute.** Catastrophic.
- **Underestimating dust + debris.** Mars rovers protected.
- **No landing site survey.** Boulders kill landers.

## Summary

- Parachutes for atmospheric capsules.
- Propulsive for boosters + Mars + lunar.
- Sky Crane for heavy rovers.
- Water + land + vacuum landings.
- Trend: propulsive everywhere, precision improving.
